# What Is a Customer Data Platform (CDP)?

**A customer data platform (CDP)** is software that collects customer data from multiple sources - online and offline, first-party and zero-party - and unifies it into persistent, individual customer profiles that other tools can access and act on. It's not a destination for data. It's a central hub that makes customer data usable across every system in the stack.

Segment, Bloomreach, Klaviyo CDP, and mParticle are among the most widely used in the e-commerce space.

## FAQs

### What is a CDP in marketing?
In marketing, a CDP is the infrastructure layer that connects a brand's data sources - Shopify for transactions, Klaviyo for email, Gorgias for support, a loyalty platform for rewards - into unified customer profiles. Those profiles power segmentation, personalization, predictive modeling, and cross-channel campaign targeting. It's the difference between marketing to a database of email addresses and marketing to a complete picture of a real customer. Most brands don't feel the gap until they're running multiple tools and nothing talks to anything else.

### What's the difference between a CDP, a CRM, and a DMP?
A CRM (Customer Relationship Management platform) focuses on managing customer relationships - it's typically sales-centric and contains manually entered or imported contact data. A DMP (Data Management Platform) works with anonymous, aggregated data from third-party sources, primarily for programmatic advertising. A CDP works with identified, first-party customer data and is built specifically to create unified profiles for marketing activation. CDPs are more real-time, more granular, and more directly tied to personalization than either. They solve a different problem.

### Do Shopify merchants need a CDP?
Not always - and not from day one. Most merchants under $1M/month can get real mileage from Shopify's native data combined with Klaviyo's built-in segmentation. The inflection point tends to come when a brand is running multiple tools - loyalty, SMS, email, support - and campaigns aren't reflecting the full customer picture. When a retention email goes to a customer who just redeemed a loyalty reward and made a purchase, that's a data fragmentation problem. And that's exactly what a CDP solves.

### How does a CDP interact with a loyalty platform?
A CDP ingests loyalty program data - points balances, tier status, redemption history, last activity date - as inputs to customer profiles. Those profiles then power smarter loyalty campaigns: winback flows that know a customer's last redemption, tier upgrade nudges timed to purchase probability scores, VIP segments that reflect true cross-channel engagement rather than just order count. The loyalty platform writes to the CDP; the CDP writes back to every activation channel. That two-way relationship is where the value compounds.

## Related Terms

- **Single customer view (SCV).** The unified customer profile that a CDP produces - one complete record per customer across all channels and systems.
- **Data silo.** The problem a CDP is built to solve - fragmented customer data spread across disconnected tools.
- **Zero-party data.** Customer-declared preference data that flows into a CDP alongside behavioral and transactional data.
- **Machine learning.** CDPs often apply machine learning to the unified profiles they hold, generating predictive scores like churn risk and purchase probability.
