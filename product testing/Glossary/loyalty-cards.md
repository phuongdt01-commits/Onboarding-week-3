# What Are Loyalty Cards?

**Loyalty cards** are physical or digital cards that identify a customer as a loyalty program member and track their purchases, points, or visit counts so they can earn and redeem rewards over time. They're the interface between the customer and the loyalty program - the thing a customer presents at checkout to log their activity.

Physical loyalty cards were the dominant format for decades, issued by supermarkets, pharmacies, and airlines. Digital versions now replicate that function inside apps, mobile wallets, and customer accounts - without the friction of carrying a physical card. Most major programs have moved primarily digital: Starbucks, Woolworths, and Target all run app-based versions. And for good reason - the data advantages alone make the switch worth it.

## FAQs

### What are loyalty cards used for in retail?
In retail, loyalty cards serve two functions: customer identification and behavior tracking. When a customer scans their card, the retailer connects that transaction to a specific individual - building a purchase history that enables personalized offers, segmentation, and churn prediction. Without a card or digital equivalent, those transactions are anonymous. The data is gone. You can't personalize for someone you don't recognize.

### What's the difference between loyalty cards and loyalty programs?
A loyalty card is the mechanism; a loyalty program is the system. The card is what a customer carries or presents - it's the identifier. The program is the full set of rules, benefits, tiers, and rewards that the card connects the customer to. You can have a loyalty program without a physical card (most ecommerce programs work this way), but a physical loyalty card can't exist without a program behind it. One is infrastructure. The other is the offering.

### Are physical loyalty cards still relevant?
Less so than they were. The shift to digital-first loyalty has been significant, driven by smartphone adoption and the data advantages of app-based tracking. That said, physical cards still perform in specific contexts - particularly grocery and pharmacy retail where older demographics are a large customer segment. For most ecommerce brands, the equivalent of a loyalty card is a customer account connected to a loyalty platform. That's where the action is.

### How do digital loyalty cards compare to physical ones?
Digital loyalty cards win on data. A physical card tells a brand that a specific customer made a purchase. A digital card or account-linked equivalent tells the brand when, what, at what price, how often, and which marketing touchpoint preceded the visit. That data gap is why brands moving from physical to digital programs consistently report better CLV insights and more effective personalization. The card format changed. The goal didn't.

## Related Terms

- **Loyalty program.** The rewards system that loyalty cards provide access to - the card is the identifier, the program is the structure.
- **Loyalty platform.** The software that manages member accounts, point balances, and redemptions - effectively the digital replacement for physical card infrastructure.
- **Points-based loyalty program.** The most common program type that loyalty cards are used to access, tracking points earned per transaction.
- **Retention rate.** The customer behavior metric that loyalty card programs are designed to improve by giving customers a reason to return.
