# What Is a Key Performance Indicator (KPI)?

**A key performance indicator (KPI)** is a measurable value that tracks how effectively a business or team is achieving a specific objective. KPIs translate abstract goals into concrete, trackable numbers - making it possible to assess progress, identify problems, and make decisions based on evidence rather than intuition.

Not every metric is a KPI. A KPI is tied to a specific goal, has an owner, and is reviewed on a defined cadence.

## FAQs

### What is a KPI in business?
In business, a KPI is a metric selected to represent progress toward a goal that matters. The distinction from a general metric is intentionality: there are hundreds of numbers a business can track, but KPIs are the ones formally designated as success indicators for a given objective. A KPI typically has a target (e.g., "achieve 4x ROAS this quarter"), a timeframe, and accountability attached to it. Without those elements, it's just a number that shows up in a dashboard and doesn't change anyone's behavior.

### What's the difference between a KPI and a metric?
All KPIs are metrics, but not all metrics are KPIs. A metric is any quantitative measure - page views, email open rate, cart abandonment rate. A KPI is a metric elevated to strategic importance because it tracks progress toward a defined business goal. A useful test: if the metric doesn't change a decision or prompt action, it probably isn't a KPI. It's just reporting. And reporting without action is just a time cost.

### What are common KPIs for e-commerce?
E-commerce teams typically track KPIs across acquisition, retention, and profitability. Common examples include ROAS (return on ad spend), customer acquisition cost (CAC), conversion rate, average order value (AOV), customer lifetime value (LTV), return rate, and gross margin. Which metrics get elevated to KPI status depends on the business's current priorities - a brand focused on profitability will weight margin KPIs differently than one focused on growth. The mix should shift as the business shifts.

### How many KPIs should a business track?
As few as possible while still covering the dimensions that matter. Most business thinkers recommend 3 to 7 KPIs per team or objective - enough to catch blind spots, not so many that focus is lost. A long list of KPIs usually means nothing is truly a priority. The power of a KPI comes from the attention and accountability attached to it, and that dilutes fast as the list grows. Pick fewer. Care more about the ones you pick.

## Related Terms

- **Gross merchandise value (GMV).** A top-line volume KPI commonly used to track e-commerce scale and growth trajectory.
- **Return on ad spend (ROAS).** One of the most common acquisition KPIs for e-commerce brands running paid advertising.
- **Customer lifetime value (LTV).** A retention-focused KPI that measures the long-run revenue contribution of each customer relationship.
- **Average order value (AOV).** A transactional KPI that tracks how much customers spend per order and feeds directly into LTV calculations.
