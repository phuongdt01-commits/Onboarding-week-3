# What Is Customer Advocacy?

**Customer advocacy** is when existing customers actively promote a brand to others - through word-of-mouth, reviews, referrals, or social sharing - without being paid to do so. It's the highest expression of customer loyalty, and it generates acquisition at a fraction of the CAC of paid channels.

Advocacy sits above loyalty in the customer relationship hierarchy. A loyal customer keeps buying. An advocate keeps buying and brings others along. Brands like Glossier and Patagonia have built large portions of their customer base on advocacy rather than advertising spend. That's not an accident - it's the outcome of years of earned trust.

## FAQs

### What is customer advocacy in marketing?
In marketing, customer advocacy refers to a brand's ability to turn satisfied customers into active promoters. It's measured by metrics like Net Promoter Score (NPS), referral rate, and user-generated content volume. Unlike influencer marketing, authentic advocacy isn't paid - it's earned through product quality, service, and emotional connection to the brand. You can't buy it. You can only build toward it.

### What's the difference between customer advocacy and customer loyalty?
Loyalty is internal - it's a customer's personal preference for your brand over alternatives. Advocacy is external - it's a customer actively telling others about your brand. Every advocate is loyal, but not every loyal customer is an advocate. The distinction matters because advocacy has a direct impact on acquisition cost, while loyalty primarily affects retention rate and CLV. They're related, but they're not the same lever.

### How do brands measure customer advocacy?
The most common proxy metric is Net Promoter Score, which asks customers how likely they are to recommend the brand on a 0-10 scale. Brands also track referral program participation rates, review volume and sentiment, and social mention share. No single metric captures advocacy fully. Most retention-focused brands track a combination, and they watch the trends more than the point-in-time numbers.

### Can small e-commerce brands build customer advocacy?
Yes - and category scale isn't the limiting factor. Brands with strong community identity, exceptional unboxing experiences, or highly specific product quality (think specialty coffee, niche apparel, or sustainable personal care) routinely build high-advocacy customer bases with modest budgets. The driver is customer experience, not marketing spend. A brand that delights its first 500 customers has a better advocacy foundation than one that reaches 50,000 with forgettable packaging and slow support.

## Related Terms

- **Brand ambassador.** A customer or public figure who actively and consistently promotes a brand - the most formalized expression of customer advocacy.
- **Referral program.** A structured mechanic that rewards customers for bringing new buyers to the brand. Often the first way merchants operationalize advocacy.
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS).** A survey-based metric that measures the share of customers likely to recommend a brand. The standard benchmark for advocacy health.
- **Customer loyalty.** The attitudinal preference a customer has for a brand. The foundation from which advocacy grows.
- **User-generated content (UGC).** Photos, reviews, and posts created organically by customers. A visible byproduct of strong customer advocacy.
