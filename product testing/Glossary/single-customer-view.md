# What Is a Single Customer View (SCV)?

**A single customer view (SCV)** is a unified, deduplicated profile of an individual customer that pulls together all data about them - purchase history, browsing behavior, email engagement, support interactions, loyalty activity - into one complete record. It gives every team access to the same understanding of who that customer is and how they've behaved across every touchpoint.

Without an SCV, customer data is fragmented. The email platform sees one slice of a customer. The support tool sees another. The loyalty platform sees a third. None of them talk to each other. And the customer ends up feeling like they're dealing with three different companies - because, effectively, they are.

## FAQs

### What does a single customer view include?
An SCV typically includes: contact details, transaction history (every order, SKU, and value), channel behavior (email opens, SMS clicks, site visits), support history (tickets, resolution time, sentiment), and loyalty data (points balance, tier, redemption history). Some SCVs also include predicted attributes - churn risk score, predicted next purchase date - generated from machine learning models run on top of the unified data. That last layer is where SCVs go from useful to genuinely powerful.

### What's the difference between a single customer view and a customer profile?
A customer profile is what most CRMs and email platforms already store - name, email, purchase count, maybe a tag or segment. An SCV is more complete. It's deduplicated (one record even if the customer used two email addresses), cross-channel (it pulls from every system, not just one), and continuously updated in near real-time. A customer profile is a snapshot. An SCV is a living record. The gap between the two is where personalization either works or doesn't.

### How does a single customer view improve marketing results?
It eliminates the gaps that make marketing feel generic. A brand without an SCV might send a "we miss you" winback email to a customer who placed an order three days ago - because the email platform didn't know about the purchase. With an SCV, every campaign, message, and loyalty touchpoint is informed by the customer's complete history. Personalization stops being aspirational and starts being operational. That shift is less dramatic than it sounds and more impactful than most brands expect.

### What tools build a single customer view?
Customer Data Platforms (CDPs) like Segment, Bloomreach, or Klaviyo CDP are built specifically to create SCVs. They ingest data from Shopify, email, support, and loyalty platforms, deduplicate records, and produce unified profiles. Some brands build lightweight versions using native Shopify data plus integrations - but dedicated CDPs handle the complexity more reliably at scale. For brands doing meaningful volume, a proper CDP isn't a nice-to-have. It's the foundation everything else depends on.

## Related Terms

- **Customer Data Platform (CDP).** The primary tool used to construct and maintain a single customer view across all channels and systems.
- **Data silo.** The fragmented state that a single customer view is designed to replace - isolated data stores that don't share information.
- **Zero-party data.** Customer-provided data that enriches the SCV with self-reported preferences and intent signals.
- **Personalization.** What becomes possible once an SCV exists - tailoring every interaction to the actual customer rather than a demographic assumption.
