# What Is a Repeat Customer?

**A repeat customer** is a person who has made more than one purchase from the same brand. They've passed the initial acquisition hurdle and converted again - making them fundamentally more valuable than a one-time buyer in terms of CLV, margin contribution, and likelihood of future purchases.

Repeat customers aren't synonymous with loyal customers. Repeat purchase behavior can be driven by convenience, subscription mechanics, or habit as much as genuine brand preference. But as a behavioral signal, repeat purchase is one of the clearest indicators of retention health - and a key input into virtually every e-commerce profitability model.

## Key Aspects

- **Decision rule:** The first 7-30 days after the initial purchase is the highest-leverage window to convert a new buyer into a repeat customer.
- **Benchmark:** Repeat purchase rate is typically tracked over a 90 or 180-day window; moving that rate from 25% to 35% has a significant compound effect on CAC efficiency over 12 months.

## FAQs

### What is a repeat customer in e-commerce?
In e-commerce, a repeat customer is typically defined as anyone with two or more completed orders in a given store. Most platforms track this automatically. The relevant metric is repeat purchase rate - the percentage of customers who return for a second order within a defined window, often 90 or 180 days. For most Shopify merchants, getting a customer to that second purchase is the most critical conversion in the entire retention journey. Everything else flows from that.

### What's the difference between a repeat customer and a loyal customer?
A repeat customer has made multiple purchases - that's a behavioral fact. A loyal customer has a genuine preference for the brand that goes beyond habit or convenience. All loyal customers are repeat customers, but not all repeat customers are loyal. Loyal customers tend to have higher average order values, stronger resistance to competitor offers, and they're far more likely to leave reviews or refer friends. The distinction matters when you're deciding where to invest.

### How often do repeat customers spend more than new customers?
Consistently more - and the gap widens over time. First-time buyers are the most expensive to acquire and often the most cautious in their spending. Customers who've placed two or three orders have already demonstrated product satisfaction. They tend to show higher basket sizes. Brands that track cohort CLV by purchase number typically see average order value increase materially between the first and third purchase. That's not a coincidence. Trust compounds.

### How do brands increase their repeat customer rate?
Post-purchase experience is the primary lever: follow-up email sequences, loyalty program enrollment, product education, and review requests all influence whether a first-time buyer comes back. The window right after the first purchase - typically the first 7 to 30 days - is when a brand has the most opportunity to convert a new buyer into a repeat one. Don't waste that window on silence.

## Related Terms

- **Repeat purchase rate.** The percentage of customers who make more than one purchase within a defined period. The core metric for tracking repeat customer behavior.
- **Customer lifetime value (CLV).** The total revenue expected from a customer over their relationship with the brand. Repeat customers form the foundation of high-CLV cohorts.
- **Customer retention.** A brand's ability to keep customers coming back. Repeat purchase rate is one of its most direct measures.
- **Loyalty program.** A structured rewards system designed to convert first-time buyers into repeat customers by giving them a reason to return.
- **Churn rate.** The percentage of customers who don't return. The inverse signal to repeat customer rate.
