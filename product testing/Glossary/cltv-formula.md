# What Is the CLTV Formula?

**Customer lifetime value (CLTV)** is a metric that estimates the total revenue a business can expect from a single customer over the entire duration of their relationship. It's one of the most important numbers in e-commerce because it sets the ceiling for how much a merchant can profitably spend to acquire and retain customers.

The standard CLTV formula is:

> Formula: CLTV = Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan

Each variable represents a lever merchants can work on independently. That's what makes it useful - it's not just a diagnostic number. It tells you where to pull.

## Key Aspects

- **Formula:** CLTV = Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan.
- **Example:** A customer spending $80 per order, buying 4 times per year, and staying active for 3 years produces a CLTV of $960.
- **Benchmark:** An LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 or higher is the standard target - generating at least $3 in lifetime revenue per $1 spent on acquisition.

## FAQs

### How is CLTV calculated?
CLTV is calculated by multiplying three numbers together: average order value (the mean amount spent per transaction), purchase frequency (how many times a customer buys per year), and customer lifespan (how many years the average customer stays active). For example, a customer who spends $80 per order, buys 4 times a year, and stays for 3 years has a CLTV of $960. Some versions of the formula subtract customer acquisition cost to get net CLTV - which is the number that actually tells you whether a customer is profitable.

### What's the difference between CLTV and LTV?
The terms are used interchangeably in most e-commerce contexts. CLTV (Customer Lifetime Value) and LTV (Lifetime Value) refer to the same calculation. Some analysts use CLV as the abbreviation instead. The underlying formula and concept don't change - it's the same metric with different shorthand depending on who's writing. Don't let the terminology variation distract you from what you're actually measuring.

### What is a good CLTV for e-commerce?
There's no universal benchmark because CLTV varies widely by product category, price point, and margin. A more useful benchmark is the LTV:CAC ratio - the ratio of customer lifetime value to customer acquisition cost. A ratio of 3:1 or higher is generally considered healthy. That means you're generating at least $3 in lifetime revenue for every $1 spent to acquire a customer. Below 2:1, the unit economics start to look concerning. That's the ratio to watch.

### How does CLTV connect to retention strategy?
CLTV is directly shaped by purchase frequency and customer lifespan - both of which retention programs are designed to increase. A loyalty program that drives a second and third purchase from someone who would have bought only once meaningfully raises that customer's CLTV. And small improvements compound fast. Moving a customer from one purchase per year to two doesn't just double purchase frequency - it often extends lifespan too, because engaged customers don't churn as readily. The math adds up quickly.

## Related Terms

- **Average order value (AOV).** One of the three inputs to the CLTV formula - the mean dollar value of a customer's transactions.
- **Purchase frequency.** How often a customer buys within a given period, another core variable in the CLTV calculation.
- **Customer acquisition cost (CAC).** The cost to acquire a new customer, typically benchmarked against CLTV to assess profitability.
- **Churn rate.** The rate at which customers stop buying - a key driver of customer lifespan and therefore CLTV.
