# What Is GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)?

**Gross merchandise value (GMV)** is the total dollar value of all goods sold through a platform or store over a given period, calculated before deducting returns, fees, or any other costs. It measures transaction volume rather than actual earnings, making it a top-line indicator of business scale rather than profitability.

GMV is calculated as:

> Formula: GMV = Number of Orders x Average Order Value

In marketplace contexts, GMV includes the total value of all transactions, even though the platform only keeps a fraction as revenue.

## Key Aspects

- **Formula:** GMV = Number of Orders x Average Order Value.
- **Benchmark:** Early-stage brands (below $1M GMV) typically target 100%+ year-over-year growth; established brands in the $10M-$100M range consider 20-50% annual GMV growth strong.
- **Rule of thumb:** GMV is a volume indicator only - pair it with gross profit and return rate to assess whether the transaction volume is actually profitable.

## FAQs

### What is GMV in e-commerce?
In e-commerce, GMV represents the gross value of all completed transactions in a store or platform during a specific timeframe. It's a raw volume figure - it doesn't account for returns, refunds, discounts, or platform fees. For a Shopify merchant, GMV is essentially the sum of all order totals before any deductions are applied. Brands use it to size a business and compare growth trajectories over time. But it's a starting point, not a destination.

### What's the difference between GMV and revenue?
GMV and revenue aren't the same thing. GMV is the total value of goods sold. Revenue is what the business actually keeps after deducting returns, platform fees, and any other reductions. For a direct-to-consumer Shopify brand, the gap between GMV and net revenue comes from refunds and chargebacks. For a marketplace like Amazon's third-party seller network, the gap is much larger - the platform only retains a commission percentage of total GMV. Knowing which number you're looking at matters.

### What is a good GMV growth rate?
GMV growth benchmarks vary significantly by stage and category. Early-stage brands below $1M in GMV often target 100%+ year-over-year growth. More established brands in the $10M-$100M range typically consider 20-50% annual GMV growth strong. But the number matters less in isolation than its relationship to profitability. High GMV growth with declining margins tells a very different story than balanced growth. That's what to watch.

### Is GMV a useful metric for Shopify merchants?
Yes, as a volume indicator. GMV tells you how much purchasing activity is happening in your store, which is useful for trend analysis and sizing decisions. But it's a misleading proxy for business health on its own. A merchant can have rising GMV while margins compress. The meaningful analysis pairs GMV with gross profit, return rate, and net revenue to get a full picture. GMV alone won't tell you if you're actually building something profitable.

## Related Terms

- **Net revenue.** What remains after returns, refunds, and fees are subtracted from GMV - the figure that appears on the income statement.
- **Average order value (AOV).** One of the two inputs to the GMV calculation, alongside order count.
- **Revenue recognition.** The accounting principle determining when GMV converts to recognized revenue on financial statements.
- **Gross profit.** GMV minus cost of goods sold - the metric that tells you whether the volume being generated is actually profitable.
