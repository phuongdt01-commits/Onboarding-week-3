# What Is Zero-Party Data?

**Zero-party data** is information that a customer intentionally and proactively shares directly with a brand - their preferences, purchase intentions, personal context, or product interests. Unlike behavioral data that brands infer from clicks and purchases, zero-party data is volunteered. The customer tells you what they want.

The term was coined by Forrester Research. It sits at the top of the data trust hierarchy precisely because there's no guesswork involved - the customer is the source. And that changes what you can do with it.

## FAQs

### What is zero-party data in marketing?
In marketing, zero-party data typically comes from quizzes, preference centers, onboarding surveys, product recommendation tools, and loyalty program sign-up flows. A skincare brand asking "What's your skin type?" during checkout. A pet brand asking "How old is your dog?" at account creation. These are zero-party data collection moments, and they're more valuable than they look. The data feeds directly into product recommendations, email segmentation, and personalized loyalty rewards - without any inference required.

### What's the difference between zero-party data and first-party data?
First-party data is behavioral - what a brand observes about a customer through its own channels (purchase history, site visits, email engagement). Zero-party data is declared - what the customer directly tells the brand. Both are owned by the brand and don't rely on third-party tracking. But the difference matters: first-party data tells you what a customer did, zero-party data tells you what they want. Inference versus declaration. One requires interpretation; the other doesn't.

### Why has zero-party data become more important?
Third-party cookies are being phased out across browsers, and iOS privacy changes have cut into ad tracking significantly. Brands that relied on third-party data for audience targeting are facing real gaps in what they know. Zero-party data doesn't depend on any of that infrastructure - it comes directly from the customer relationship. For brands building loyalty programs, every quiz completion, preference update, and survey response is a zero-party data event that makes future personalization more accurate.

### How do loyalty programs collect zero-party data?
Loyalty programs are one of the best collection mechanisms available because customers are already motivated to engage. Brands collect zero-party data by offering points for completing a preference quiz, asking about product interests during tier onboarding, or prompting members to update their profile for a birthday reward. The exchange is explicit: the customer shares something, the brand rewards them for it. That's a fundamentally different dynamic than tracking behavior behind the scenes.

## Related Terms

- **First-party data.** Behavioral data collected directly by the brand through its own channels - complementary to zero-party data but inferred rather than declared.
- **Customer Data Platform (CDP).** Centralizes zero-party and first-party data into unified customer profiles used for segmentation and personalization.
- **Personalization.** The primary use case for zero-party data - tailoring recommendations, emails, and loyalty rewards to self-reported preferences.
- **Single customer view (SCV).** The unified customer record that zero-party data enriches, adding declared preferences to observed behavioral history.
