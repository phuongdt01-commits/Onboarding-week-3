# What Is Advocate Share Rate?

**Advocate share rate** is the percentage of loyalty program members who actively refer the brand to others - through a referral link, social share, or direct recommendation - within a given period. It measures how many of your customers act as active promoters, not just passive repeat buyers.

It sits at the intersection of loyalty and acquisition. A high advocate share rate means the existing customer base is doing some of the work of growing itself - reducing dependence on paid channels and lowering effective CAC across new customer cohorts.

## Key Aspects

- **Benchmark:** E-commerce programs with meaningful referral incentives typically target 10-20% advocate share rate among active loyalty members.
- **Rule of thumb:** Advocate share rate counts only customers who take a referral action, making it a more demanding signal than NPS, which measures intent only.
- **Decision rule:** When advocate share rate is high but referral conversion rate is low, the problem is in the referral offer or targeting, not in loyalty health.

## FAQs

### What is advocate share rate in marketing?
In marketing, advocate share rate is a loyalty metric that tracks active promotion behavior - not sentiment. A customer can be satisfied and never share. Advocate share rate counts only the ones who take action: clicking "share," sending a referral link, completing a referral event. It's a more demanding signal than NPS, which only measures intent to recommend. And it's more specific than word-of-mouth, which is difficult to attribute cleanly. Advocate share rate gives you an actual number - not an intention.

### What's the difference between advocate share rate and referral conversion rate?
Advocate share rate measures the share of members who initiate a referral or share action. Referral conversion rate measures how many of those referrals result in a new customer acquisition. Related but distinct. A brand can have a high advocate share rate with a low referral conversion rate - many shares, few converts - which points to a targeting or offer problem in the referral mechanic, not a loyalty problem. Diagnosing which one is broken matters for knowing where to fix it.

### What is a good advocate share rate benchmark?
Advocate share rate varies significantly by industry, program structure, and whether the brand actively incentivizes sharing. Programs with explicit referral rewards (a discount for both referrer and referred) typically see higher rates than programs relying on organic sharing alone. In e-commerce, a functional referral program with meaningful incentives often targets 10-20% advocate share rate among active loyalty members. Brands where identity and community are core - fitness, beauty, sustainability - tend to see rates at the high end of that range.

### Does advocate share rate affect customer acquisition cost (CAC)?
Yes, and the math compounds. Referral-driven customers are cheaper to acquire than customers from paid channels - they arrive with brand awareness already established, often with a conversion incentive, and tend to have higher retention rates and CLV than cold-traffic customers. A rising advocate share rate doesn't just lower CAC in the current period. Each cohort of referred customers has its own potential to advocate, which means a strong referral program can generate compounding acquisition at a fraction of the paid CAC. That's the real leverage here.

## Related Terms

- **Referral program.** The loyalty mechanic that directly drives advocate share rate by incentivizing members to share with their networks.
- **Customer acquisition cost (CAC).** The cost to acquire a new customer; advocate share rate is the metric most directly linked to reducing it through organic referral activity.
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS).** A survey-based measure of recommendation intent; advocate share rate is the behavioral counterpart - it tracks what customers actually do, not what they say they'd do.
- **Emotional loyalty.** The underlying customer attachment that motivates unprompted advocacy; high emotional loyalty typically produces a higher organic advocate share rate.
