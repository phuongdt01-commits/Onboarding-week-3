# What Is Repeat Purchase Rate?

**Repeat purchase rate** is the percentage of customers who make more than one purchase from a brand within a given time period. It's a direct measure of customer loyalty and one of the most reliable leading indicators of long-term CLV growth.

Also called repurchase rate, it sits at the center of any retention-focused strategy. A high repeat purchase rate means customers find enough value to come back on their own. A low one means the brand is running on first-time buyers - and paying CAC for every order. That's a costly treadmill, and repeat purchase rate is the number that tells you whether you're on it.

## Key Aspects

- **Formula:** Repeat purchase rate = customers who purchased more than once / total customers, within a defined time period.
- **Benchmark:** A repeat purchase rate above 25-30% is generally considered healthy for DTC e-commerce; top-performing brands with strong loyalty programs often reach 40-50%.
- **Rule of thumb:** Measure within a window that matches the actual repurchase cycle for the product category, not a universal 30 or 90-day default.

## FAQs

### What is repeat purchase rate in e-commerce?
In e-commerce, repeat purchase rate is typically measured over a 30, 90, or 365-day window. The right window depends on the product category and expected repurchase cycle. A coffee brand might measure it at 30 days. A skincare brand at 90. A home goods brand might look at a full year. Measuring across the wrong window produces misleading numbers - a 30-day repeat rate for a mattress brand will always look bad, not because retention is poor but because the product doesn't need replacing monthly. Choose the window that matches the actual buying cycle.

### What's the difference between repeat purchase rate and customer retention rate?
These measure related but distinct things. Repeat purchase rate focuses on whether a customer buys again - it's binary. Retention rate looks at whether customers who were active in one period are still active in the next - it captures ongoing engagement over time. Both are useful. But repeat purchase rate is a simpler, cleaner signal for understanding whether first-time buyers convert into returning ones. Start there before adding complexity.

### What is a good repeat purchase rate for e-commerce?
For DTC e-commerce, a repeat purchase rate above 25-30% is generally considered healthy. Top-performing brands with strong loyalty programs often see rates above 40-50%. Shopify data has consistently shown that the top 1% of stores by revenue generate a large proportion of their orders from repeat buyers. The exact benchmark varies by category - consumable products like supplements, pet food, and personal care tend to see higher rates than durable goods. Compare yourself against your category, not some universal average.

### How do you calculate repeat purchase rate?
Repeat purchase rate = customers who purchased more than once / total customers, within a defined time period. If 500 out of 2,000 customers in a quarter bought at least twice, the repeat purchase rate is 25%. Some brands use an even simpler version: second-purchase rate - measuring specifically how often first-time buyers return for a second order. That first repeat purchase is the critical conversion in a customer's lifecycle. Get that number right, and everything downstream improves.

## Related Terms

- **Customer retention rate.** The percentage of customers who remain active over time. Closely related to repeat purchase rate but captures ongoing engagement rather than a single repeat transaction.
- **Purchase frequency.** The average number of times a customer buys within a period. Repeat purchase rate tells you who comes back; purchase frequency tells you how often.
- **Customer lifetime value (CLV).** Repeat purchase rate is one of the core inputs into CLV - more repeat buyers means higher average lifetime revenue per customer.
- **Churn rate.** The inverse signal to repeat purchase rate. High churn and low repeat purchase rate often move together.
