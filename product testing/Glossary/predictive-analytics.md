# What Is Predictive Analytics in Marketing?

**Predictive analytics** is the use of historical data, statistical algorithms, and machine learning to forecast future outcomes - what a customer is likely to do next, when they'll buy again, or whether they're about to churn. In e-commerce, it turns past behavior into actionable forward-looking signals that inform campaigns, loyalty strategies, and retention decisions.

It's distinct from descriptive analytics, which explains what already happened. Predictive analytics answers: what's going to happen next?

## FAQs

### What is predictive analytics in marketing?
In marketing, predictive analytics generates scores or forecasts at the individual customer level. Common outputs include: churn probability (how likely is this customer to stop buying?), predicted customer lifetime value (how much will this customer spend over the next 12 months?), next purchase date (when will they likely order again?), and product affinity (which products are they most likely to buy?). These scores are used to trigger campaigns, personalize content, and prioritize marketing spend. The goal isn't just prediction - it's action that wouldn't have happened without it.

### What's the difference between predictive analytics and descriptive analytics?
Descriptive analytics tells you what happened - your average order value last quarter, your repeat purchase rate by cohort. Predictive analytics tells you what's likely to happen next: which customers will churn in the next 30 days, which segments are most likely to respond to a loyalty tier promotion. Descriptive is backward-looking. Predictive is forward-looking. Most brands use both, but the retention advantage gets built on the predictive side.

### How accurate is predictive analytics?
Accuracy depends on data quality and volume. Models trained on thousands of transactions with complete behavioral signals can produce churn predictions accurate enough to meaningfully improve retention campaign ROI. Models trained on sparse or fragmented data produce much noisier outputs. Klaviyo publishes their predictive accuracy benchmarks - their churn risk model uses 180 days of purchase behavior as a baseline. The more complete the customer data, the sharper the predictions. There's no shortcut around the data quality problem.

### How do loyalty programs use predictive analytics?
Loyalty programs generate dense behavioral data - redemption patterns, point accrual rates, tier movement, engagement frequency - that predictive models use heavily. Brands use those outputs to trigger pre-churn loyalty campaigns before a customer lapses (not after), to identify VIP candidates before they reach a tier threshold, and to predict which reward types will drive the highest redemption rate for a given segment. That's the difference between reactive retention and proactive retention. And proactive wins.

## Related Terms

- **Machine learning.** The underlying technology that powers most modern predictive analytics models - the system that identifies patterns in historical data.
- **Customer lifetime value (CLV).** One of the primary outputs of predictive analytics - a forward-looking estimate of how much a customer will spend over time.
- **Customer churn rate.** Predictive analytics is often used specifically to identify customers at high risk of churning before they actually do.
- **Customer Data Platform (CDP).** Centralizes the customer data that predictive models are trained and run on - the data foundation predictive analytics depends on.
