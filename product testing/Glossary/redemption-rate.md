# What Is Redemption Rate in Loyalty Programs?

**Redemption rate** is the percentage of loyalty points, rewards, or vouchers that customers actually redeem, out of all points or rewards issued. It's one of the most direct signals of whether a loyalty program is genuinely engaging customers or just sitting unused in the background.

Redemption rate = points redeemed / points issued. A low redemption rate doesn't mean the program is cost-efficient - it usually means customers don't see enough value in the rewards to use them. That's a program design problem, not a good outcome.

## Key Aspects

- **Formula:** Redemption rate = Points redeemed / Points issued.
- **Healthy range:** 15-25% of points issued annually ([Bond Brand Loyalty](https://bondbrandloyalty.com)). Below 10% signals program design problems. Above 35-40% creates budget pressure.
- **Rule of thumb:** If your threshold requires more than three purchases to reach, expect redemption below 10%.

## FAQs

### What is redemption rate in marketing?
In loyalty marketing, redemption rate is the clearest proxy for program engagement. A loyalty program can show impressive enrollment numbers while hiding a much deeper problem: members who earn points but never redeem them aren't actually engaged - they're disengaged customers who signed up once and never came back for more. High redemption rate means customers understand the program, value the rewards, and are motivated by it to return. It's the behavioral proof that a program actually works. Enrollment numbers aren't.

### What's the difference between redemption rate and earn rate?
Earn rate is the rate at which customers accumulate points - typically expressed as points per dollar spent. Redemption rate measures whether those accumulated points are ever used. A brand can have a high earn rate (generous points per purchase) and a low redemption rate (rewards nobody wants or can figure out how to use). The gap between the two is called breakage - points earned but never redeemed. Some breakage is normal and expected. Too much signals a disengaged member base, and that's a problem worth fixing.

### What is a good redemption rate for a loyalty program?
Healthy loyalty programs typically target redemption rates of 15-25% of points issued per year, according to [Bond Brand Loyalty's annual Loyalty Report](https://bondbrandloyalty.com), one of the most widely cited benchmarks in the industry. Rates below 10% often indicate members don't find the rewards compelling or the redemption process is too complicated. Rates above 35-40% can put real cost pressure on the program budget. Starbucks Rewards is often cited as a high-redemption benchmark - the program is structured to make redemption easy, and the rewards (free drinks) are tangible enough that members actually want them. That combination is harder to achieve than it looks.

### How do you improve redemption rate?
Redemption rate improves when rewards feel attainable and desirable. The two most common failure modes are thresholds set too high (customers give up before reaching them) and reward options that don't match what customers actually want. A useful rule of thumb: if your redemption threshold requires more than three purchases to reach, expect redemption rates below 10%. Simplifying the path to redemption - fewer steps, points balance visible at checkout - consistently moves the number up. Starbucks Rewards is built around this principle: most members can redeem within two purchases at standard price points, and the program's low-threshold design is consistently cited by loyalty analysts as a model for driving redemption engagement. Once you've simplified the path, the next question is whether the reward itself is worth the effort. Both halves of that problem need solving.

## Related Terms

- **Breakage.** Points or rewards that are issued but never redeemed. High breakage can look like profit margin but signals low program engagement.
- **Earn rate.** The rate at which customers accumulate points in a loyalty program. The counterpart to redemption rate in measuring program economics.
- **Loyalty program.** The broader structure within which redemption rate is measured. Redemption rate is one of the clearest indicators of a program's health.
- **Customer retention rate.** Redemption rate and retention rate tend to move together - when customers are redeeming rewards, they're typically also returning to buy.
