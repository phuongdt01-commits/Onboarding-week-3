# What Is Accrued Revenue?

**Accrued revenue** is income that a business has earned by delivering goods or services but hasn't yet collected payment for. It's recorded as an asset on the balance sheet - the company is owed money, even though no cash has changed hands yet. Under accrual accounting, this revenue appears in the income statement in the period it was earned.

The concept is the opposite of deferred revenue, where cash is received before the obligation is fulfilled. That distinction - earned but unpaid versus paid but unearned - is worth keeping clear. Both affect how revenue flows through your financials, and confusing them is an easy way to misread your own income statement.

## FAQs

### What is accrued revenue in accounting?
In accounting, accrued revenue is a type of receivable - specifically, revenue that has been recognized under the accrual method but hasn't been invoiced or paid yet. It's recorded with a debit to an asset account (like "accrued revenue" or "unbilled revenue") and a credit to a revenue account. The entry reverses when the cash is actually received or the invoice is issued. If you've delivered the goods but the invoice goes out 30 days later, that gap is where accrued revenue lives.

### What's the difference between accrued revenue and deferred revenue?
Accrued revenue means you've done the work but haven't been paid yet. Deferred revenue means you've been paid but haven't done the work yet. They sit on opposite sides of the balance sheet: accrued revenue is an asset, deferred revenue is a liability. Both concepts come from the same underlying principle - revenue should be recognized when earned, not when cash moves. Same rule, opposite situations.

### Does accrued revenue apply to e-commerce businesses?
It depends on the business model. Pure transactional e-commerce - where customers pay at checkout before shipment - rarely generates accrued revenue. It becomes relevant for merchants running net payment terms with wholesale buyers, subscription services where delivery spans a period, or B2B arrangements where invoices are sent after fulfillment. Gift card breakage can also create accrued revenue scenarios. Most standard Shopify stores won't deal with it often, but it matters when they do.

### Is accrued revenue the same as accounts receivable?
They're closely related but not identical. Accounts receivable is money owed after a formal invoice has been issued. Accrued revenue is money owed before an invoice exists - it's been earned but not yet billed. Once an invoice is sent, accrued revenue typically converts to accounts receivable on the balance sheet. Think of accrued revenue as the stage before accounts receivable. Same underlying obligation, different point in the billing cycle.

## Related Terms

- **Deferred revenue.** Payment received before a service or product has been delivered - the mirror image of accrued revenue.
- **Revenue recognition.** The accounting principle that determines when revenue is recorded, which governs whether revenue is accrued or deferred.
- **Accounts receivable.** Formally invoiced but unpaid amounts owed to a business - the stage accrued revenue moves into once billed.
- **Accrual accounting.** The accounting method that makes accrued revenue a meaningful concept, requiring revenue to be recorded when earned rather than when cash arrives.
