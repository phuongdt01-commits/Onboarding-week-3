# What Is LTV? How to Calculate Customer Lifetime Value

**Customer lifetime value (LTV)** is a metric that estimates the total revenue a business will generate from a single customer over the entire course of their relationship. It's the foundational number behind decisions about how much to spend on acquisition, what to invest in retention, and how to segment customers by profitability.

The standard LTV calculation formula is:

> Formula: LTV = Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan

A more refined version accounts for profit rather than revenue:

> Formula: LTV = (Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan) x Gross Margin

## Key Aspects

- **Formula:** LTV = Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan; profit-adjusted version multiplies that result by Gross Margin.
- **Formula:** Profit LTV = (AOV x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan) x Gross Margin.
- **Benchmark:** A healthy LTV:CAC ratio is 3:1 or higher; below 1:1 means acquisition spend exceeds total customer revenue.

## FAQs

### How is LTV calculated for e-commerce?
The basic LTV calculation multiplies three variables: average order value (AOV), purchase frequency (orders per customer per year), and customer lifespan (how many years a customer stays active). A customer who spends $75 per order, places 3 orders per year, and stays for 2 years has an LTV of $450. The profit-adjusted version multiplies that figure by gross margin - so a 50% margin gives a profit LTV of $225. That second number is the one that actually matters for unit economics decisions.

### What's the difference between LTV and CLTV?
They're the same metric. LTV (lifetime value), CLTV (customer lifetime value), and CLV (customer lifetime value) all refer to the same calculation. Different teams use different abbreviations, but the formula and interpretation don't change. Some analysts add "C" for "customer" to distinguish it from other types of lifetime value calculations, but in e-commerce contexts it's the same number. Don't let the terminology variation trip you up.

### What is a good LTV for an e-commerce brand?
LTV is most useful in ratio form. A healthy LTV:CAC ratio - lifetime value compared to customer acquisition cost - is typically 3:1 or higher. A ratio below 1:1 means you're spending more to acquire customers than they'll ever generate in revenue. That's not sustainable. For Shopify merchants in the $50K-$500K/month range, the specific LTV number matters less than whether it's trending up and whether it exceeds acquisition costs by a meaningful margin. The trend line tells you more than the snapshot.

### How does LTV differ by customer cohort?
LTV isn't a single number across all customers. It varies by acquisition channel, first product purchased, geography, and a lot of other factors. Customers acquired through word-of-mouth typically have higher LTV than those acquired through paid ads. Customers who make a second purchase within 90 days of their first tend to have significantly higher LTV than one-time buyers. Cohort-level LTV analysis is far more actionable than a store-wide average. The average hides the variation; the variation is where the decisions live.

## Related Terms

- **Average order value (AOV).** One of the three core inputs to the LTV calculation - the mean spend per transaction.
- **Customer acquisition cost (CAC).** The benchmark against which LTV is measured - the LTV:CAC ratio is the clearest signal of acquisition efficiency.
- **Churn rate.** The rate of customer attrition that directly determines customer lifespan in the LTV formula.
- **Purchase frequency.** How often a customer buys per year - the variable most directly influenced by retention programs and loyalty strategies.
