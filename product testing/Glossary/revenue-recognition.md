# What Is Revenue Recognition?

**Revenue recognition** is the accounting principle that governs when revenue is recorded on your financial statements - specifically, the point at which a sale is considered "earned" rather than just collected. For e-commerce merchants, understanding this distinction is critical for accurate reporting and tax compliance.

Revenue is recognized when goods or services are transferred to the customer, not necessarily when payment is received. That gap between collection and recognition shapes how your income statement looks month to month. And for growing merchants, it's one of the gaps that tends to create the most confusion.

## Key Aspects

- **Rule of thumb / Decision rule:** Under ASC 606, revenue is recognized when a performance obligation is satisfied - for most e-commerce merchants, that is at shipment, not at payment.
- **Decision rule:** Subscription revenue is recognized over the service period; gift card revenue is recognized at redemption, with breakage rules applied to cards that are never used.

## FAQs

### What is revenue recognition in accounting?
Revenue recognition in accounting refers to the rules that define when a company can officially record a sale as revenue. Under the widely adopted ASC 606 standard, revenue is recognized when a performance obligation is satisfied - meaning when the customer receives what they paid for. For a Shopify merchant, that's typically when an order ships, not when the customer clicks "Buy." The timing difference seems small. It compounds quickly at scale.

### What's the difference between revenue recognition and cash accounting?
Cash accounting records revenue the moment payment is received. Revenue recognition under accrual accounting records it when the product or service is delivered. A merchant who collects payment today but ships next month will recognize that revenue next month - not today. The two methods can produce very different profit figures for any given period. Neither is wrong inherently; they just answer different questions about the business.

### When is revenue recognized for e-commerce?
For most e-commerce businesses, revenue is recognized at the point of shipment or delivery - whichever transfers control of the goods to the buyer. Subscriptions and gift cards are treated differently: subscription revenue is recognized over the service period, and gift card revenue is recognized when the card is redeemed (with breakage rules for cards that are never used). Gift card breakage alone is an area where many merchants aren't accounting correctly.

### Why does revenue recognition matter for growing merchants?
It matters because reported revenue and cash in the bank are often two different numbers. Merchants scaling fast can look profitable on paper while facing cash flow problems - or vice versa. Accurate revenue recognition gives a true picture of business performance, which matters for investor reporting, loan applications, and making sound financial decisions. Get it wrong and your P&L is telling you a story that isn't real.

## Related Terms

- **Accrued revenue.** Revenue that has been earned but not yet collected in cash - the other side of the recognition timing gap.
- **Deferred revenue.** Payment received before the goods or service have been delivered, recorded as a liability until the obligation is fulfilled.
- **Cost of goods sold (COGS).** The direct costs tied to the products whose revenue is being recognized in a given period.
- **Gross merchandise value (GMV).** The total value of orders processed - a figure that's often confused with recognized revenue but is not the same thing.
