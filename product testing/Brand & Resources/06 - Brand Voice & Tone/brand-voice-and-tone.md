# Joy Loyalty — Brand Voice & Tone

## Overview

Joy's voice is built for ambitious merchants who move fast and think big. It's authoritative but never stiff, motivational but never fluffy, and always direct. Every word should feel intentional and earned.

---

## Four Voice Dimensions

### 1. Strategic / Authoritative
Joy speaks like an expert who has seen what works and what doesn't. It doesn't speculate — it advises. Always links features back to high-level business outcomes. Focuses on the "why" and "what if."

- **Do:** "Loyalty programs that tie rewards directly to repeat purchase behavior generate 3x more Assisted Orders."
- **Don't:** "Loyalty programs can sometimes lead to more repeat purchases."

| DOs | DON'Ts |
|-----|--------|
| Back features and opinions with statistics, trustworthy research, analytics, or reports. *eg: "48% increase in customer lifetime value encouraged by rewards"* | Use general words or claim generic statements without proof or evidence |
| Connect features/updates to the north-star metric (customer retention). Emphasize the purpose. *eg: "Unlock repeat purchases and growth with Memberships"* | Only describe features technically, without connecting to business outcomes |
| Use vocabulary the target audience already knows | Use lengthy academic terms or presume the audience will understand jargon |

---

### 2. Motivational
Joy pushes merchants to reach for more. The tone is energizing, not cheerleading. It's a coach giving a pre-game talk — not a fan in the stands. Challenges the status quo and inspires action.

- **Do:** "You've built a strong product. Now build the retention engine that makes customers stay."
- **Don't:** "Loyalty programs are a great way to make your customers feel appreciated!"

| DOs | DON'Ts |
|-----|--------|
| Always include the most powerful possible outcomes customers can achieve. Focus on the **transformation.** *eg: "No more restrictions. Build whatever loyalty programs for your brand. Now!"; "2x repeat purchases with diversified reward programs"* | Avoid talking lightly about what customers can achieve or downplaying results |
| Use strong, positive statements that motivate action. Believe in a better outcome. | Write passive, hedge-filled copy that fails to inspire action |
| Tell heroic stories. Use the motif: clear villain (challenge/struggle) + belief in victory (solution) + clear triumph/transformation | Default to feature-first explanations that skip the merchant's story |

---

### 3. Direct & High-Paced
No throat-clearing. No filler sentences. Get to the point fast. Avoids lengthy preambles — uses punchy, active verbs and short sentences suitable for busy, driven executives.

- **Do:** "Most loyalty platforms limit your program structure. Joy doesn't."
- **Don't:** "When it comes to loyalty programs, there are many different platforms out there that offer various features, and it can be hard to choose the right one for your business."

| DOs | DON'Ts |
|-----|--------|
| Cut all unnecessary words, flowery language, and long explanations. It should be quick to read and understand. | Write lengthy sentences or explanations. Prioritize shorter, faster versions |
| Focus on command and action. Use strong verbs and imperative sentences. *eg: "Stop imagining. Start executing your unique loyalty programs with Joy now"* | Write passive or tentative copy that hedges the call to action |
| Use **bolding**, CAPITAL letters, short paragraphs, and clear bullet points when appropriate | Bury the key message in a wall of text |
| Use exclamation points, dashes, and periods aggressively when needed | Write run-on sentences padded with transitional filler |

---

### 4. Confident
Joy doesn't hedge. It doesn't apologize for being bold. Confidence comes from proof — so statements should be backed by data or specificity. Poised, composed, direct, and slightly authoritative.

- **Do:** "Joy-powered brands see an average 28% increase in repeat purchase rate within 90 days."
- **Don't:** "Joy might help improve your repeat purchase rates over time."

| DOs | DON'Ts |
|-----|--------|
| Use strong, active verbs and state facts clearly. *eg: "Joy delivers the most flexible and powerful loyalty platform on the market"* | Use conditional or tentative language (*might, maybe, perhaps, we think, we hope*) |
| Focus on results and guarantees. Highlight mastery and successful outcomes (with data) | Overpromise what Joy is not yet able to deliver |
| Use inclusive language ("us", "together", "we") to show Joy is a partner. *eg: "Succeed together. Rise together."* | Be too loud, braggy, or aggressive about Joy's achievements — confidence is quiet |

## Brand Vocabulary

These are Joy's approved power words. Use them deliberately — not in every sentence, but regularly enough to reinforce the brand voice.

### Power Action Words
| Word | Use Case |
|------|----------|
| **Crush** | "Crush your churn rate" |
| **Outperform** | "Outperform the competition" |
| **Conquer** | "Conquer customer retention" |
| **Dominate** | "Dominate your category" |
| **Command** | "Command customer loyalty" |

### Capability Words
| Word | Use Case |
|------|----------|
| **Arsenal** | "Your retention arsenal" |
| **Weapon** | "The most powerful weapon in your retention stack" |
| **Battle-tested** | "Battle-tested loyalty mechanics" |
| **Unrestricted** | "Unrestricted program design" |
| **Limitless** | "Limitless customization" |

### Quality Words
| Word | Use Case |
|------|----------|
| **Superior** | "Superior loyalty mechanics" |
| **Mastery** | "Achieve mastery over retention" |
| **Absolute** | "Absolute control over your program" |

### Metric Words *(always preferred over vague language)*
| Word | Use Case |
|------|----------|
| **ROI** | "Prove loyalty ROI with Assisted Orders data" |
| **CLV** | "Increase customer lifetime value" |
| **Assisted Orders** | Joy's core metric — every feature should connect to this |
| **Retention rate** | "Drive higher retention rate" |
| **CAC** | "Lower your customer acquisition cost" |

---

## Words to Avoid

These undermine Joy's brand voice:

| Avoid | Replace With |
|-------|-------------|
| "Help" (weak) | "Drive," "power," "enable" |
| "Boost" (vague) | "Increase by X%," "maximize" |
| "Nice" / "Great" | Be specific — show proof |
| "Try" (uncertain) | "Use," "deploy," "activate" |
| "Might" / "Could" | State with conviction |
| "Easy" (minimizing) | "Flexible," "intuitive," "frictionless" |
| "Small business" | "Ambitious brand," "growing store" |

## Tone Variations by Content Type

| Content Type | Tone Emphasis |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Blog posts** | Strategic + Motivational — teach and inspire |
| **Product pages** | Authoritative + Confident — prove and convert |
| **CTAs** | Direct + Confident — trigger action |
| **Case studies** | Authoritative + Motivational — prove and inspire |
| **Email** | Direct + High-Paced — get to value fast |
| **Social media** | Motivational + Confident — punch hard in few words |

---

## Quick Voice Test

Before publishing any content, ask:
1. Does this sound like advice from an expert, or information from a generalist?
2. Does this push the reader to think bigger?
3. Did I cut every filler word and hedging phrase?
4. Does this connect back to ROI, CLV, or Assisted Orders?
5. Would an ambitious merchant feel understood — or talked down to?
