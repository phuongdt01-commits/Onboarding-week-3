# Joy Loyalty — Core Values

## Overview

Joy's four core values define how the company operates internally and how it shows up for merchants. These values should be felt in every piece of content, every product decision, and every customer interaction.

---

## The Four Values

### 1. Competence
> Deep expertise in loyalty mechanics and e-commerce growth

Joy earns its place by knowing loyalty better than anyone. The team understands program design, retention science, and Shopify's ecosystem deeply — and that expertise shows up in the product and in every piece of content.

**What this means for content:**
- Every article should demonstrate genuine expertise — not surface-level coverage
- Cite real data, real mechanics, real best practices
- Never publish content that a competitor could copy word-for-word

---

### 2. Resilience
> Built to help brands survive and thrive through challenges

Loyalty is a long game. Joy is built for merchants who are in it for the long term — and the brand reflects that same staying power. Resilience means Joy doesn't promise quick fixes; it delivers sustainable systems.

**What this means for content:**
- Frame loyalty as a long-term strategy, not a short-term tactic
- Acknowledge the difficulty of building retention — then show how to overcome it
- Avoid "get rich quick" framing around loyalty program results

---

### 3. Integrity
> Transparent, honest, accountable

Joy doesn't oversell. If a feature has a tradeoff, say so. If a strategy takes time to show results, be upfront. Integrity builds trust — and trust is what loyalty programs are fundamentally about.

**What this means for content:**
- Back up claims with real data — no vague superlatives
- Be honest about what loyalty programs can and can't do
- Don't use manipulative urgency or false scarcity in CTAs

---

### 4. Dedication
> Long-term partner mindset, not a transactional tool

Joy doesn't see merchants as customers — it sees them as partners. The relationship is long-term, and every product decision is made with merchant success as the north star.

**What this means for content:**
- Write for the merchant's long-term success, not just the immediate conversion
- Educational content should genuinely teach — not just funnel toward a sale
- Position Joy as a growth partner, not just a software subscription

## How Values Show Up in Brand Voice

The four values and four voice dimensions work together:

- **Competence** → Strategic/Authoritative voice
- **Resilience** → Determination personality trait
- **Integrity** → Confident voice (earned, not assumed)
- **Dedication** → Motivational voice
