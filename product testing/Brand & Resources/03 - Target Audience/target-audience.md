# Joy Loyalty — Target Audience

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> **Ambitious & driven-minded Shopify store owners who are ready to stop acquiring and start retaining.**

---

## Demographics & Business Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Mindset** | Ambitious, driven, performance-oriented |
| **Platform** | Shopify |
| **Business size** | Mid-to-Enterprise |
| **Monthly revenue** | $50,000 – $500,000 |
| **Monthly ad spend** | $10,000+ |
| **Monthly order volume** | 400+ orders |
| **Geography** | Asia, USA, European Union |

## Customer Pain Points

These are the frustrations that bring merchants to Joy:

| Pain Point | What It Means |
|------------|---------------|
| **High CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | They're spending too much getting new customers and need to extract more value from existing ones |
| **Low retention rate** | Customers buy once and don't come back; there's no loyalty loop |
| **Generic programs** | Off-the-shelf loyalty programs don't reflect their brand or stand out to their customers |
| **Technical limitations** | Other platforms can't do what they want — Joy's flexibility solves this |

---

## What the ICP Wants

- A loyalty program that looks and feels like *their* brand, not a white-label template
- Real ROI data — they track Assisted Orders, CLV, and retention rate
- A platform that scales with them as they grow
- Speed — they move fast and need tools that keep up
- Control — they don't want to depend on a support team to make changes

---

## Content Implications

When writing for Joy's audience:

- **Don't baby them** — they're experienced operators, not beginners
- **Lead with metrics** — they think in revenue, not in "engagement"
- **Assume high ambition** — they want to dominate their category, not just "improve"
- **Respect their time** — direct, no-fluff writing is essential
- **Speak their language** — CLV, CAC, retention rate, AOV, assisted orders
