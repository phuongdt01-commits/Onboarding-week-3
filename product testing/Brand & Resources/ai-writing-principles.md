# AI Writing Principles

These principles work alongside Joy's brand voice to create content that is both authentic and effective.

## Critical Principles

### 1. Evidence-Based and Honest

Build trust through transparency and specificity. This is foundational.

**Cite specific sources and data:**
- ✅ "According to Semrush's latest report, only 12% of companies appear as trusted citations."
- ❌ "Research shows most companies struggle with visibility."

**Acknowledge strengths AND weaknesses:**
- ✅ "While Joy doesn't support multi-currency points calculation yet, here's how merchants handle it using Shopify Markets."
- ❌ "Joy provides comprehensive multi-currency support."

**Call out limitations honestly:**
- ✅ "One thing to be aware of: custom code additions may affect support availability."
- ❌ "We provide excellent support for all customization needs."

**Use precise numbers:**
- ✅ "Shopify gives you 24 free templates. Squarespace gives you 194."
- ❌ "Squarespace offers significantly more templates."

**Include real examples:**
- ✅ "For example, stores using Flow can automatically tag VIP customers when they hit $1,000 in lifetime spending."
- ❌ "You can set up automated tagging for VIP customers."

**Explain methodology:**
- ✅ "We tested this with five different store configurations to verify it works across themes."
- ❌ "This works for all stores."

### 2. Direct and Clear

Get to the point. Readers shouldn't wade through fluff.

**Lead with verdict or key takeaway:**
- ✅ "At $5/month, Shopify Starter is one of the cheapest ways into ecommerce - but it doesn't include a standalone store."
- ❌ "In today's competitive landscape, businesses seek cost-effective solutions. After considering various factors including pricing, features, and scalability, we determined that Shopify Starter, available at $5/month, may suit certain use cases, though limitations exist."

**Front-load paragraphs:**
- ✅ "Points-per-dollar programs work best for repeat purchase businesses."
- ❌ "In evaluating various loyalty program structures, we've found that certain approaches tend to perform better in specific contexts. For instance, programs that reward based on spending..."

**Cut unnecessary words:**
- ✅ "Getting mentioned is half the battle. Getting cited is the other half."
- ❌ "It could be argued that getting mentioned is essentially half of what might be considered the battle."

**Use short, punchy sentences for impact:**
- ✅ "That's Flow in action."
- ❌ "This represents what we refer to as Shopify Flow, which is a strategic automation tool."

### 3. Professional but Conversational

Sound like a knowledgeable friend, not a corporation.

**Avoid corporate speak:**
- ✅ "If you want a full online store, Shopify wins - hands down."
- ❌ "For organizations seeking to leverage robust ecommerce solutions to optimize their digital transformation, Shopify represents a paradigm-shifting platform."

**Use everyday language:**
- ✅ "Here's the problem: customers need to see value quickly."
- ❌ "The contemporary engagement paradigm necessitates immediate value perception."

**Address the reader directly:**
- ✅ "Maybe you've already launched your program. Maybe you're still planning."
- ❌ "Many merchants have launched programs. Astute planners recognize the importance."

**Share personal testing:**
- ✅ "When I tested the referral widget, I found it took about 5 minutes to set up."
- ❌ "The referral widget features an intuitive setup process."

**Use contractions naturally:**
- ✅ "You're not just building a program. You're building relationships."
- ❌ "You are not just building a program. You are building relationships."

### 4. Personal Writing Quirks (Non-Negotiable)

These distinguish authentic writing from AI-generated content:

- Spell "okay" not "ok"
- Write "email" not "e-mail"
- Use ellipses... when creating a pause for thought
- Capitalize Random Words for Emphasis (sparingly)
- Always spell out numbers under ten
- "Five staff accounts" not "5 staff accounts"

### 5. Banned Phrases

Never use these - they kill authenticity instantly:

**Corporate buzzwords:**
- ❌ "synergize", "leverage" (as verb), "utilize" (say "use")
- ❌ "streamlined", "robust", "seamless"
- ❌ "cutting-edge", "game-changing", "revolutionary"
- ❌ "empower", "transform", "unlock" (as marketing speak)
- ❌ "paradigm shift", "best-in-class", "world-class"

**Tired phrases:**
- ❌ "at the end of the day"
- ❌ "take your business to the next level"
- ❌ "dive deep"
- ❌ "low-hanging fruit"
- ❌ "move the needle"

**Passive hedging:**
- ❌ "it should be noted that"
- ❌ "it is important to mention"
- ❌ "arguably"
- ❌ "to be fair"

**Empty openers:**
- ❌ "In today's digital landscape..."
- ❌ "In the world of ecommerce..."
- ❌ "When it comes to loyalty programs..."

## Important Principles

### Structure and Organization

**Use informative subheadings:**
- ✅ "Why Points Expire: The Psychology Behind It"
- ❌ "Overview"

**Group related information:**
Keep related concepts together. Don't scatter setup steps across sections.

**Use white space:**
Break up text with short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max).

**Lead with examples, follow with explanation:**
- ✅ "Set points to expire after 180 days. This creates urgency..."
- ❌ "Creating urgency is important. One way to do this is by setting expiration..."

### Comparisons

**Use tables for direct comparisons:**
Don't force prose when a table is clearer.

**Acknowledge tradeoffs:**
- ✅ "Shopify has more apps but steeper learning curve. Squarespace is simpler but less flexible."
- ❌ "Shopify is the better platform."

**Give "best for" guidance:**
End comparisons with clear recommendations for different use cases.

### Practical Examples

**Use realistic scenarios:**
- ✅ "A coffee shop might offer a free drink after 10 purchases."
- ❌ "Businesses can create reward structures."

**Show before/after:**
When explaining changes or improvements, show the contrast.

**Include edge cases:**
Don't just explain ideal scenarios. Address common problems.

## Refinement Principles

### Emphasis and Rhythm

**Use italics for emphasis (not bold):**
- ✅ "This is *really* important for high-volume stores."
- ❌ "This is **really** important for high-volume stores."

**Vary sentence length:**
Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones.

**End sections with impact:**
Use a one-sentence paragraph for final thought.

### Lists and Bullets

**Use numbered lists for steps/rankings:**
When order matters or there's a clear sequence.

**Use bullet points for features/options:**
When items are equal in importance.

**Limit to 5-7 items per list:**
Break longer lists into sections.

**Make each item parallel:**
Start with same part of speech (verbs or nouns).

### Calls to Action

**Be specific:**
- ✅ "Go to Program Settings > Rewards to add your first reward."
- ❌ "Set up your rewards."

**Explain the benefit:**
- ✅ "Start with a simple points-per-dollar program - it's easiest to explain to customers."
- ❌ "Start with points-per-dollar."

## Integration with Joy Voice

When writing for Joy:

1. **Start with Joy's voice attributes** (humble, knowledgeable, supportive, clear, inspirational)
2. **Apply these writing principles** to ensure quality and authenticity
3. **Adapt for context** (UI needs brevity, help articles need structure, etc.)

The result: Content that sounds like Joy AND follows proven writing practices.
