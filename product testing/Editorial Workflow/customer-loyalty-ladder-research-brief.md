## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent
Informational-commercial hybrid. Users seek to understand the loyalty ladder framework to justify retention strategy investment AND identify software to automate it. Secondary intent: comparing ladder models (5 vs. 6 vs. 7 rungs) to find the best fit.

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems
- **High acquisition costs:** Acquiring new customers costs 5–7x more than retaining existing ones (industry benchmark — needs primary source verification)
- **One-time buyer churn:** "Leaky bucket" problem where 50%+ of new customers never return
- **Ineffective personalization:** Generic marketing fails to account for customer's loyalty stage
- **Revenue concentration:** 65% of revenue often comes from small fraction of loyal repeat customers (80/20 rule — needs primary source verification)
- **Tracking & attribution gap:** Can't see which rung each customer occupies or what drives upward movement

### What this article MUST achieve
1. **Explain the ladder model** — define the concept, show 3–4 popular frameworks (5-rung, 6-rung, 7-rung), recommend which fits 7-figure Shopify merchants
2. **Connect rungs to revenue** — show how moving customers up the ladder directly impacts CLV, repeat purchase rate, referral revenue (Assisted Orders)
3. **Provide actionable transition tactics** — specific strategies to move customers from Prospect → 1st-Time → Repeat → Loyal → Advocate
4. **Emphasize data & personalization** — highlight why tracking rung placement + personalized messaging is essential (Joy's differentiator)
5. **Position Joy** — show how Joy's flexibility (custom rungs, multi-channel automation, referral rewards) outpaces rigid competitors
