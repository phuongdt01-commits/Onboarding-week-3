# **Best Practices for Leveraging AI Tools in Blog Post Writing**

Using AI tools like ChatGPT, and Claude can make your blog writing process faster and more efficient. Here are some best practices to ensure you’re getting the most out of these tools while maintaining quality and originality:

1. **Use AI for Brainstorming Ideas**  
   * Generate a list of potential topics, titles, or outlines to jumpstart your creativity.  
   * Ask the AI for keyword ideas or popular questions related to your topic.  
2. **Draft with AI, Refine with Your Voice**  
   * Use AI to create the initial draft, but always edit and refine to ensure it aligns with your brand’s tone and voice.  
   * Add your unique perspective, examples, and expertise to make the content stand out.  
3. **Fact-Check and Verify Accuracy**  
   * While AI is great for generating content, always verify facts, data, and citations to ensure accuracy.  
   * Include up-to-date information from credible sources to enhance credibility.  
4. **Tailor Content for SEO**  
   * Prompt the AI to suggest keywords and incorporate them naturally into the text.  
   * Optimize for search engines by asking the AI to refine headlines, meta descriptions, or image alt text.  
5. **Don’t Skip Personalization**  
   * Add anecdotes, case studies, or unique insights that AI cannot replicate.  
   * Customize the content to address the specific needs and pain points of your target audience.  
6. **Stay in Control of the Process**  
   * Use AI as a tool, not a replacement for your creativity and judgment.  
   * Ensure every piece of content is authentic, valuable, and aligned with your goals.

# **1\. “How to” Blog Post** {#1.-“how-to”-blog-post}

Whether it’s “how to make chicken parm” or “how to start a business,” people are searching “how to” do things on Google all the time. A “how to” blog post allows you to meet this demand and provide your audience with a step-by-step guide to solving a problem or achieving a goal.

“How to” blog posts are an excellent way for your company to position itself as a trustworthy, customer-focused resource. By laying out tactical approaches to fixing problems or addressing needs without asking for anything in return, you establish your brand as helpful and knowledgeable.

Writing these posts can also help your blog rank for Google’s featured snippet — the box that appears in search results providing a quick answer to a query. Below, we’ll guide you through the steps to create an effective “how to” blog post that resonates with your readers and performs well in search engines.

“How to” blog posts are ideal for:

* Explaining equations or calculations \[e.g., “How to Calculate Net Promoter Score”\].  
* Providing a framework for addressing a challenging task \[e.g., “How to Set & Achieve Marketing Objectives”\].  
* Offering clear, step-by-step instructions for straightforward tasks \[e.g., “How to Block Websites on Chrome Desktop and Mobile”\].

# **2\. Newsjacking Blog Post** {#2.-newsjacking-blog-post}

Newsjacking blog posts allow you to harness the momentum of a trending news story and connect it to your audience in a meaningful way. By framing breaking news in a way that resonates with your readers, you can position your brand as timely, relevant, and insightful.

Newsjacking is the practice of aligning your content with the popularity of a news story to increase visibility and drive engagement. Originally coined by David Meerman Scott in his book Newsjacking: How to Inject Your Ideas into a Breaking News Story and Generate Tons of Media Coverage, this approach works best when executed carefully and thoughtfully.

There are two key ways to approach newsjacking: offering an immediate rundown of what’s happening or a reflective analysis of the news and its implications. You could also consider publishing an initial post and updating it as the story develops. Regardless of the approach, clarity, accuracy, and sensitivity are crucial—especially if the topic is sensitive or polarizing. Below is a template to help you craft an effective and impactful newsjacking blog post.

Newsjacking blog posts are ideal for:

* Breaking down complex news stories relevant to your audience \[e.g., “How the Latest Data Privacy Law Impacts Small Businesses”\].  
* Offering actionable advice in response to current events \[e.g., “What to Do After the Google Algorithm Update”\].  
* Establishing thought leadership by providing insights on industry news \[e.g., “Why the Recent Supply Chain Disruptions Matter for E-commerce”\].

# **3\. List Blog Post** {#3.-list-blog-post}

We all love countdowns, rankings, and lists – including your readers. This presents an unignorable opportunity for your blog team: list posts.

List blog posts are exactly what they sound like – a blog post listing off examples, resources, or tips pertaining to a topic your readers will love, are interested in, or would benefit from knowing more about. List posts can range from as low as three to as high as 100+, though the sweet spot that most bloggers gravitate towards tends to be between five and 20\.

Another perk of the list approach to blog posts is that it is appropriate for every stage of the  buyer’s journey. As an example, a digital marketing agency could see success with an awareness post titled “The 10 Social Media Trends Your Company Can’t Ignore” and with a decision stage post titled “3 Qualities to Look For in a Marketing Agency.”

List blog posts are ideal for:

* Examples \[e.g., “8 of the Best Professional Bio Examples We've Ever Seen \[+ Bio Templates\]”\].  
* Steps \[e.g., “3 Steps to Do Your Best Work, No Matter Where You Are in Your Career”\].  
* Tips \[e.g., “19 Tips to Leave the Perfect Sales Voicemail”\].  
* Ways to Do Something \[e.g., “10 Impressive Ways to Start a Cover Letter \[+ Examples\]”\].  
* Ideas \[e.g., “31 Secret Santa Gift Ideas Your Coworkers Will Love”\].  
* Statistics \[e.g., “23 Remarkable Twitter Statistics to Be Aware of”\].  
* Facts \[e.g., “9 Interesting Facts About List Posts”\].  
* Myths \[e.g., “The 20 Most Dangerous Sales Myths You Shouldn't Fall For”\].

# **5\. Pillar Blog Post** {#5.-pillar-blog-post}

A pillar page is designed to be the authoritative resource for a given topic on the internet. Unlike standard blog posts, which might focus on lists or how-to guides, a pillar page should serve as the ultimate, all-encompassing guide on a subject. Readers should leave your page feeling like they have everything they need to know about the topic.

Pillar pages are supported by related blog posts, known as “cluster” posts, which link back to the pillar page. This pillar-cluster model not only improves the reader experience but also strengthens your site’s SEO by signaling to search engines that your page is the go-to resource for the topic. If this model is new to you, check out HubSpot’s [guide](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/pillar-cluster-model-transform-blog) on how we rolled it out across our blog.

Pillar pages are typically more in-depth than standard posts, often ranging from 2,000 to 5,000 words. To maintain reader engagement, include interactive elements like videos, embedded social media posts, or calculators to break up the text. These pages should also incorporate keywords and backlinks strategically to solidify their authority.

Here are a few examples of pillar pages:

* [The Ultimate Guide to Video Marketing](https://blog.hubspot.com/marketing/video-marketing)  
* [The Ultimate Guide to Entrepreneurship](https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/entrepreneurship)  
* [The Ultimate Guide to Software as a Service](https://blog.hubspot.com/service/saas)

Pillar blog posts are ideal for:

* Establishing your site as an authoritative resource on a topic.  
* Organizing related blog content into a cohesive structure for better SEO.  
* Providing in-depth, evergreen content that attracts and retains readers.

# **6\. “What Is” Blog Post** {#6.-“what-is”-blog-post}

How often do you find yourself typing “what is \[blank\]” into Google? Weekly? Daily? Hourly?

Sometimes, your readers just need a quick answer to a question. A “what is” blog post allows you to address these queries while providing additional context for those who want to dive deeper. By answering these questions succinctly, you not only serve your audience but also increase your chances of ranking for Google’s featured snippet—the box that provides quick answers at the top of search results.

“What is” blog posts can also take the form of “when is,” “who is,” or “why is” posts. The same general structure and guidelines apply to these variations.
