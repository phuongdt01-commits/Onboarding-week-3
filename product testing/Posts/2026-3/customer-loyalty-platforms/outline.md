## 3. H2: The 8 Evaluation Criteria (What Separates Winners from Waste)

**Word count:** 900-1100 words
**Writing structure:** Criterion definition → Why it matters → ROI impact → How to evaluate

**H3 mini-roadmap:**

### 3.1: Integration Capabilities (APIs, Shopify, POS, CRM)

**Word count:** 120-150 words

**Key claims:**
- 75% of loyalty platform value comes from integration
- Shopify-native platforms cut implementation time by 50%
- Bidirectional sync (loyalty ↔ CRM/email) is table stakes

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** Bad integrations = manual data entry + inconsistent customer view + missed upsell signals
- **What to evaluate:** Shopify API depth, CRM sync, email marketing hooks, POS/app connectivity
- **Red flags:** "Custom integration required", "manual CSV exports", no API documentation
- **Key claim:** Integration speed is ROI speed; poor integrations = months of waste

---

### 3.2: ROI Measurement & Analytics Dashboards

**Word count:** 120-150 words

**Key claims:**
- 83% of program owners report positive ROI; most can't prove it without good dashboards
- Real dashboards show: revenue lift, repeat purchase rate, AOV increase, churn reduction
- Opaque analytics = flying blind

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** You need to see: Customer LTV by segment, program-attributed revenue, referral performance, redemption patterns
- **What to evaluate:** Pre-built ROI dashboards, revenue attribution, segment performance breakdown, real-time reporting
- **Red flags:** "Custom reports only", data delayed by 24+ hours, no revenue attribution
- **Key claim:** Measurement is management; if you can't see ROI, it doesn't exist

---

### 3.3: Implementation Speed & Ease

**Word count:** 120-150 words

**Key claims:**
- Fast deployment = faster ROI; 90-day platforms go live in 4-8 weeks; rigid platforms take 3-6 months
- No-code customization means you iterate without developer overhead
- Onboarding quality directly correlates with 6-month retention

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** Slow launches = longer cash burn + customer frustration + early churn risk
- **What to evaluate:** Days to first launch, no-code rule builder, responsive onboarding, pre-built templates
- **Red flags:** "3-month implementation", "developer required for customization", "manual configuration"
- **Key claim:** Agile platforms win; rigid ones lock you into month-long deployment cycles

---

### 3.4: Data Security & Compliance (GDPR, ISO, Audit Trails)

**Word count:** 100-130 words

**Key claims:**
- 31% of customers switch after one poor support/security experience
- GDPR compliance is non-negotiable; ISO certifications indicate serious security posture
- Enterprise customers will reject platforms without audit trails

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** Customer data = your responsibility; breach = brand death + legal liability
- **What to evaluate:** GDPR compliance, ISO certifications, SOC 2 Type II, encryption, audit trails, data residency options
- **Red flags:** "We're working on compliance", no certifications, unclear data handling
- **Key claim:** Security is baseline; don't negotiate on this

---

### 3.5: Customer Support Quality

**Word count:** 100-130 words

**Key claims:**
- Support quality directly correlates with platform success; poor support = 6-month failure rate 40%+
- SLAs matter; responsiveness matters; onboarding quality matters
- 31% of customers will switch after one poor support experience

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** When deployment hits friction, you need help within hours, not days
- **What to evaluate:** Support SLAs (response time, resolution time), onboarding support, technical responsiveness, community/resources
- **Red flags:** "Support only via email", no SLAs, slow response times (48+ hours)
- **Key claim:** Platform support determines whether you succeed or struggle during launch

---

### 3.6: Reward Flexibility & Customization

**Word count:** 110-140 words

**Key claims:**
- One-size-fits-all rewards = mercenary customers; flexible rewards = apostle evolution
- Points, tiers, referrals, experiential rewards, tier bonuses — different brands need different levers
- Rigid reward models lock you into transactional forever

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** Your rewards strategy is your retention strategy; flexibility = evolution
- **What to evaluate:** Points earning/redemption rules, tiered programs, referral mechanics, bonus levers, experiential rewards
- **Red flags:** "Fixed points formula", "limited tier options", "no custom reward types"
- **Key claim:** Flexible rewards = ability to shift from transactional to emotional loyalty

---

### 3.7: Omnichannel Capabilities (Web, App, POS, In-Store)

**Word count:** 110-140 words

**Key claims:**
- 80% of loyalty adoption is now mobile-first; POS integration unlocks in-store programs
- Fragmented channels = friction; unified earning/redemption = adoption
- Omnichannel programs drive 2-3x higher engagement than web-only

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** Customers expect to earn online, spend in-store (or vice versa); siloed platforms frustrate them
- **What to evaluate:** Mobile app quality, POS integration (in-store earning/redemption), web/app sync, notification timing
- **Red flags:** "Web-only", "app planned for later", "POS integration requires custom work"
- **Key claim:** Omnichannel is no longer premium; it's expected

---

### 3.8: AI-Driven Personalization & Smart Segmentation

**Word count:** 110-140 words

**Key claims:**
- 71% of consumers expect personalized interactions; generic offers fail
- AI-driven rule engines let you segment + offer WITHOUT hiring data scientists
- Smart segmentation = 3-5x lift in offer redemption vs. broadcast offers

**Content breakdown:**
- **Why it matters:** Manual segmentation = slow, rigid; AI segmentation = real-time, adaptive, targeted
- **What to evaluate:** Rule engine flexibility (conditions, actions, automation), segment-based offers, behavioral triggers, predictive churn scoring
- **Red flags:** "Manual segment creation only", "no automation", "limited rule flexibility"
- **Key claim:** Personalization is competitive; basic platforms won't keep up

## 4. H2: The Evaluation Framework (How to Score Any Platform)

**Word count:** 300-350 words
**Writing structure:** Framework intro → Scoring method → Real example

**Content brief:**

**Key claims:**
- Rate platforms on 8 criteria (0-10 each) weighted by YOUR priority
- Different brands weight differently (SMB vs. Enterprise, simple vs. complex)
- Framework separates hype from substance

**Content breakdown:**

**Framework (Table in article):**
| Criterion | Weight (Example) | Scoring (0-10) | ROI Impact |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| Integration Capabilities | 25% | [Your platform score] | Slow integration = slow ROI |
| ROI Measurement & Analytics | 20% | [Your platform score] | Can't measure = can't improve |
| Implementation Speed | 20% | [Your platform score] | Months wasted = costs compounded |
| Data Security & Compliance | 15% | [Your platform score] | Non-negotiable baseline |
| Customer Support Quality | 10% | [Your platform score] | Enables successful launch |
| Reward Flexibility | 5% | [Your platform score] | Enables evolution |
| Omnichannel Capabilities | 5% | [Your platform score] | Expected by customers |
| AI-Driven Personalization | [varies] | [Your platform score] | 3-5x offer lift |

**How to use it:**
- Weight by YOUR priority (e.g., E-commerce SMB = prioritize Integration + Implementation Speed; Enterprise = prioritize Security + Analytics)
- Score each platform (ask for demos, trial data, customer references)
- Total score = weighted average; 80+ = strong fit; 60-79 = workable; <60 = risky

**Key claim:** This framework transforms platform evaluation from intuition to data-driven decision

## 5. H2: Implementation Checklist (From Selection to Launch)

**Word count:** 250-350 words
**Writing structure:** Pre-selection → Selection → Launch → Optimization

**H3 mini-roadmap:**

### 5.1: Pre-Selection (Weeks 1-2): Clarify Your Goals

**Word count:** 100-120 words

**Key claims:**
- Before demos, clarify what you're optimizing for (revenue lift, churn reduction, AOV, repeat purchase rate)
- Wrong goal = wrong platform choice

**Content breakdown:**
- **Steps:**
  1. Quantify current state: churn rate, repeat purchase rate, AOV, CAC
  2. Set 12-month goal: "Reduce churn by 10%", "Increase repeat purchase rate 25%", "Drive 5% revenue lift"
  3. Identify integration needs: CRM, email, POS, app
- **Output:** 1-page goals doc + integration map

---

### 5.2: Selection (Weeks 3-5): Demos, Trials, References

**Word count:** 100-120 words

**Key claims:**
- Run formal demos with scoring rubric (not casual calls)
- Ask for trial periods with real data
- Talk to 2-3 customer references (same industry + revenue tier)

**Content breakdown:**
- **Steps:**
  1. Request demos from top 3 candidates (scored pre-screening)
  2. Use evaluation framework (Section 4) to score each demo
  3. Request 2-4 week trial with your data
  4. Call 2-3 references (ask about implementation speed + support quality + ROI timeline)
- **Output:** Scorecard + reference feedback

---

### 5.3: Launch Preparation (Weeks 6-8): Planning & Kickoff

**Word count:** 80-100 words

**Key claims:**
- Good platforms have structured onboarding; use it
- Prepare your team (marketing, ops, analytics) for configuration
- Set clear launch milestones

**Content breakdown:**
- **Steps:**
  1. Kickoff call with vendor (timeline, dependencies, team roles)
  2. Map your customer data to platform fields
  3. Configure initial rules, rewards, tiers (start simple)
  4. Plan launch date + communication
- **Output:** Implementation calendar + team roles

---

### 5.4: Launch & Optimization (Weeks 9-12): Go Live, Monitor, Iterate

**Word count:** 70-100 words

**Key claims:**
- Don't wait for perfect to launch; iterate after go-live
- Monitor key metrics daily for first 4 weeks
- Add features based on customer behavior + analytics

**Content breakdown:**
- **Steps:**
  1. Soft launch to segment (10-20% of customers)
  2. Monitor: enrollment rate, offer acceptance, redemption rate, repeat purchase lift
  3. Iterate: fix friction, adjust offer mix, personalize based on data
  4. Full launch once soft launch metrics are solid
- **Output:** Launch playbook + optimization backlog

## 7. H2: Mistakes to Avoid (Platform Choice Pitfalls)

**Word count:** 200-250 words
**Writing structure:** Common mistake → Why it fails → Right approach

**Content brief:**

**Key claims:**
- 4 common mistakes waste 6-12 months + $20K-$50K
- Avoid them by sticking to the evaluation framework

**Common mistakes:**
1. **Choosing by price alone** — Cheap platform = slow implementation + poor analytics + weak support = 0 ROI; good platform = 15-25% ROI within 90 days
2. **Picking an enterprise platform for SMB needs** — Over-engineered, over-priced, 6-month implementation (overkill for growing brands)
3. **Settling for "good enough" integration** — Poor integration = manual work + data silos + missed personalization = ROI stays flat
4. **Ignoring support quality during selection** — Support-light platforms fail during launch (when you need help most)

**Right approach:**
- Evaluate all 8 criteria (not just price or feature count)
- Match platform complexity to your maturity level
- Demand strong integration upfront
- Talk to customer references about support quality
