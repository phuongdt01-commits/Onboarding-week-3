## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search Intent

**Primary:** Comparison and selection — users want to discover and evaluate open-source loyalty platforms to determine fit for their business size, tech stack, and customization needs.

**Secondary:**
- Technical architecture exploration — interest in API-first, headless, self-hosted deployment models
- Cost analysis — comparing "free" open-source against SaaS subscription costs
- Vendor independence — seeking alternatives to rigid SaaS loyalty providers

### User Context (ICP) = Core User Problems

The searcher is likely a **technical decision-maker** (CTO, dev-leaning founder, or technical PM) at a brand doing $50K–$500K/month who:

- Feels **locked in** by their current SaaS loyalty provider's limitations
- Has **in-house developers** and wants full customization control
- Is evaluating the **build vs. buy** decision for loyalty infrastructure
- Wants to understand **real costs** beyond the "free" price tag
- Needs to justify the choice to stakeholders with **ROI and risk data**

### What This Article MUST Achieve

1. **Expose the reality of the open-source loyalty landscape** — most "open-source" options are abandoned, pivoted to commercial, or require paid backends. Searchers will be surprised.
2. **Provide an honest TCO framework** — help readers calculate the true cost of self-hosting vs. SaaS (developer time, infrastructure, security, maintenance)
3. **Clarify technical concepts** — API-first, headless loyalty, composable architecture — that the SERP hints at but never explains
4. **Position Joy as the smart middle ground** — flexible enough for ambitious brands without the overhead of self-hosting open-source code
5. **Give actionable decision criteria** — a framework readers can use to decide if open-source is right for them

## 8/ Notes for Writer

### Key Platforms to Cover (Honestly)

**Actual open-source options (with caveats):**
- **Open Loyalty** — The most-cited name, but the open-source version is essentially abandoned. GitHub repo: PHP/Symfony, 24 stars, ~2021 last activity. Company pivoted to commercial cloud (openloyalty.io). Open-source code exists but is unmaintained.
- **Voucherify Loyalty Accelerator** — Open-source Next.js frontend + POS simulator. Built on composable architecture (Segment, Braze integrations). **Caveat:** Requires Voucherify's paid API backend — not truly independent open-source.
- **Various small GitHub projects** — Scattered repos with <200 stars, mostly proof-of-concept or abandoned. No production-ready alternative exists.

**The honest reality:** The "open source loyalty platform" category is largely a ghost town. Most brands searching for this will end up choosing either (a) a commercial platform like Joy that offers flexibility without the maintenance burden, or (b) building custom from scratch.

### Joy Positioning Strategy

Joy is NOT open-source, and we should NOT pretend it is. Instead, position Joy as solving the **same problems** that drive people to search for open-source:
- **Customization** → Joy's flexible configuration, API access, customizable reward widgets
- **No vendor lock-in** → Shopify ecosystem, data portability
- **Cost transparency** → Clear pricing vs. hidden TCO of self-hosting
- **Speed to value** → Minutes to set up vs. months of development
- **Maintained & secure** → SOC2/GDPR handled by the platform, not your team

### Article Structure Recommendation

**Format:** Strategic guide (NOT a listicle) — differentiates from every competitor
**Angle:** "The honest guide to open-source loyalty platforms — what the SERP won't tell you"
**Tone:** Authoritative, direct, data-backed — the reader should feel like they got the real story

### Technical Concepts to Define

1. **API-first architecture** — the loyalty engine exposes all functionality via APIs; the frontend is decoupled
2. **Headless loyalty** — no built-in UI; you build your own customer-facing experience
3. **Composable architecture** — mix-and-match best-of-breed tools (CDP + CEP + loyalty engine)
4. **Self-hosted vs. cloud-hosted** — who manages the infrastructure and at what cost

### Brand Examples to Research/Include

- Starbucks (built custom loyalty from scratch — massive investment)
- Domino's (custom-built, frequently cited in loyalty case studies)
- Brands that tried open-source and switched to SaaS (if any case studies found)
