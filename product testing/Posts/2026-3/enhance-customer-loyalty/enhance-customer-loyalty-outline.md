### **[STEP 1] Map Your Churn — Find the Bleeding Points**

**Wordcount:** 450 words
**Image after this section:** Churn funnel or customer journey bottleneck visualization (1526×779px) — showing where customers drop off

**Purpose:** Data-driven foundation (don't build programs blindly)
**Key message:** You can't fix what you can't see. Identify WHERE customers drop off + WHY they leave.

**Scope:**
- Define "churn" for your business (no purchase in 90 days? 6 months?)
- Analyze your customer journey: where are bottlenecks?
- Calculate current churn rate (metric formula)
- Segment: which customers are at-risk? (purchase frequency ↓, time since last order ↑)
- Talk to lost customers: exit surveys, win-back outreach

**Why it matters:**
- 64% of shoppers ignore brand names — you need data on individual behavior, not assumptions
- Early churn detection = cheaper intervention

**Joy positioning:** "Joy's analytics dashboard shows you customer segments + purchase velocity in real-time, so you spot at-risk groups before they disappear."

### **[STEP 2] Design Your Loyalty Program — Points, Tiers & Rules**

**Wordcount:** 450 words
**Image:** None (text-focused step)

**Purpose:** Build the mechanical foundation for repeat behavior
**Key message:** Structured rewards drive habit. But one-size-fits-all doesn't work.

**Scope:**
- Decide: points-based, tiered, status-based, or hybrid?
- Define earning mechanics: points per dollar? Bonus points for referrals?
- Set redemption thresholds: how many points = $X discount?
- Build tiers: Bronze → Silver → Gold (what perks at each level?)
- Launch strategy: soft launch vs bang (internal testing first)

**Why it matters:**
- Programs that are easy to understand + easy to redeem drive engagement
- Tiered programs create aspirational loyalty (customers climb tiers = repeated engagement)
- 68% report loyalty to brands — your job is to make it *sticky* via structure

**Joy positioning:** "Joy's flexible rule engine lets you build custom point structures + tier rewards without coding. Test, optimize, scale."

### **[STEP 6] Win Back Dormant Customers — Before You Lose Them**

**Wordcount:** 450 words
**Image:** None (text-focused step)

**Purpose:** Churn happens; reactivation is cheaper than acquisition
**Key message:** Use data to predict churn + intervene with personalized win-back tactics.

**Scope:**
- Identify inactive segments: 0 purchases in 6 months, declining order frequency, at-risk signal
- Create win-back campaigns: "We miss you" messaging + compelling incentive (exclusive discount, bonus points, free shipping)
- Timing: send before they're *completely* gone (90 days inactive, not 12 months)
- Measure: reactivation rate, cost per reactivated customer, reactivated customer LTV
- Referral angle: make win-back offers shareable (referral codes) to turn reactivation into acquisition

**Why it matters:**
- Reactivation is 5–10x cheaper than new customer acquisition (implied by CAC vs retention ROI)
- Data-driven timing = higher response rates
- Reactivated customers + referral incentive = growth + retention

**Joy positioning:** "Joy's churn prediction + automated win-back workflows identify at-risk customers + trigger personalized reactivation offers in minutes, not weeks."

### **[STEP 7] Measure ROI — Prove Loyalty Works**

**Wordcount:** 400 words
**Image after this section:** ROI breakdown chart (1526×779px) — Assisted Orders math (program cost vs net profit, retention lift examples)

**Purpose:** Track metrics that prove loyalty impact to leadership + investors
**Key message:** "If you can't measure it, you can't improve it." Track CLV + Assisted Orders relentlessly.

**Scope:**
- Calculate Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): (Avg Purchase Value) × (Purchase Frequency) × (Lifespan)
- Measure program ROI: (Assisted Orders × Avg Order Value) - (Program Cost) = Net Profit
- Track leading indicators: repeat purchase rate, churn rate, NPS
- Benchmark: compare loyal cohorts vs non-loyal cohorts (retention, LTV, repeat rate)
- Refine: A/B test tier structures, reward mechanics, messaging + optimize monthly

**Why it matters:**
- 5% retention ↑ = 25–95% profit ↑ — but only if you measure it
- CAC pressure = merchants obsess over cost per order; loyalty ROI proves value
- Monthly optimization = continuous improvement

**Joy positioning:** "Joy's 'Assisted Orders' metric directly ties loyalty program impact to revenue. You see ROI instantly: orders from referral links + loyalty-generated codes."
