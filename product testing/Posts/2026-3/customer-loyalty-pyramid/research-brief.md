## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent

**Primary:** Educational/Informational — Users seeking definition + breakdown of loyalty pyramid stages

**Secondary:**
- Strategic Application — How to transition customers between stages
- Industry-Specific Guidance — How pyramid models apply to their sector (hospitality, banking, e-commerce)

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems

- **Stealth Attrition:** Customers leaving invisibly without feedback or complaints
- **Resource Allocation Inefficiency:** Spending equal marketing effort on unprofitable vs. high-value customers
- **Lack of Customer Segmentation:** Not understanding where customers sit in the loyalty journey
- **Movement Tactics Gap:** Not knowing how to move customers from lower (awareness/trial) to higher (loyalty/advocacy) stages
- **Industry Ambiguity:** Different industries apply pyramid models differently; unclear which model fits their business

### What this article MUST achieve

1. **Define the pyramid clearly** — explain core stages (Awareness → Interest → Trial → Preference → Loyalty OR Unaware → Trier → Adopter → Loyal → Advocate)
2. **Show why it matters** — connect each stage to business profitability & retention ROI
3. **Provide diagnostic framework** — help readers identify where their customers are stuck (main gap)
4. **Show movement tactics** — what specific actions move customers UP the pyramid
5. **Address industry specifics** — hospitality vs. banking vs. e-commerce application differences
6. **Explain stealth attrition** — why customers leave quietly and how the pyramid helps detect it
7. **Connect to emotional loyalty** — emphasize that top pyramid tiers require emotional connection, not just transactional value

## H/ Notes for Writer

### Core Factors to Cover (7)
1. **Pyramid Stages** — Define the model (8-segment or 5-stage; explain both)
2. **Emotional vs. Transactional Drivers** — Why top tiers require emotional connection (52% more valuable stat)
3. **80/20 Profit Distribution** — How Pareto Principle applies (20% of customers = 80% of profit)
4. **Stealth Attrition Detection** — How pyramid helps identify customers leaving invisibly
5. **Industry-Specific Tactics** — How hospitality, banking, e-commerce differ in application
6. **Movement Tactics** — Specific actions to move customers up stages
7. **Customer Segmentation & Profiling** — How to identify which stage each customer occupies

### Content Positioning
- **Type:** Applied Practitioner's Playbook (theory + tactics, NOT just framework)
- **Angle:** Diagnostic Tool (help readers identify where customers are stuck + why they churn)
- **Focus:** Stages of loyalty + industry specifics
- **Audience:** Broad appeal (any ambitious brand/business owner, NOT just Shopify)

### Key Claims to Make
1. The pyramid is a diagnostic tool for identifying customer stuckness, not just a theoretical model
2. Emotional loyalty (top tiers) drives 52% more value + 306% higher lifetime value
3. Small retention improvements (5%) yield massive profit gains (95% in some cases)
4. Stealth attrition happens because companies don't track where customers sit on the pyramid
5. Industry-specific implementation is critical (banking ≠ hospitality ≠ e-commerce)
6. Resource allocation should follow pyramid logic: invest most in moving mid-tier customers UP, not acquiring new ones

### Stats to Prioritize
- **McKinsey 78%** (personalization moves customers up)
- **HBR 52%** (emotional connection value)
- **Bain 5% → 95%** (retention ROI)
- **Pareto 80/20** (resource allocation logic)

### Joy Features to Highlight (if applicable)
- Loyalty program mechanics that move customers UP the pyramid
- Segmentation tools to identify which tier each customer occupies
- Personalization/reward mechanics that build emotional connection (not just transactional)
- Analytics to detect stealth attrition signals
