## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent

**Primary:** Informational/Educational — users want to understand what loyalty marketing is, why it matters, and how it works as a strategic discipline.

**Secondary:**
- Strategic/Implementation — actionable steps and frameworks to build a loyalty marketing strategy
- Benchmarking/Inspiration — compare program types and see real-world brand examples
- Problem-Solving — combat rising CAC, overcome loyalty fatigue, improve retention through personalization

**Dominant intent:** Comprehensive "how-to" guide that defines the category while providing strategic frameworks for increasing customer lifetime value.

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems

- **Rising CAC pressure:** Customer acquisition costs up 60% in five years — merchants lose $29 per new customer acquired (SimplicityDX, 2022)
- **Loyalty fatigue:** Customers engage only for transactional rewards, switch for better deals. Overall brand loyalty declined from 77% (2022) to 69% (2024)
- **Data gaps:** Need first-party/zero-party data as third-party cookies phase out; Gartner predicts 1 in 3 businesses without a loyalty program will add one by 2027 for data collection
- **Points-only trap:** Two-thirds of established loyalty programs fail to deliver value (McKinsey, 2021) — merchants need to move beyond transactional "Do/Get" to emotional "Feel/Do"
- **No clear playbook:** Mid-size brands ($50K–$500K/mo) lack a structured approach — most examples in competitors are Amazon, Starbucks, REI (enterprise-scale)

### What this article MUST achieve

1. **Define loyalty marketing** as a strategic discipline (not just "run a points program") — distinguish from general retention/CRM
2. **Cover the psychology** of why customers stay loyal (intrinsic vs extrinsic motivation, overjustification effect, self-determination theory) — biggest content gap
3. **Address loyalty fatigue** head-on — why points-only fails, how to build emotional connection
4. **Provide a strategy framework** — step-by-step implementation that mid-size Shopify brands can follow
5. **Show mid-size brand examples** — not just Amazon/Starbucks; focus on brands at $50K–$500K/mo scale
6. **Position Joy** as the platform that enables both transactional AND emotional loyalty (Assisted Orders, flexible program design)

### Psychology angle (our differentiation)
- **Self-Determination Theory (SDT):** Intrinsic motivation (personal values, identity, enjoyment) vs extrinsic motivation (points, discounts, cashback)
- **Overjustification effect:** When external rewards are too salient/controlling, they REDUCE intrinsic motivation to engage with the brand. Key insight from Kim (2017): autonomy-supportive rewards preserve intrinsic motivation; controlling rewards destroy it.
- **Reciprocity principle (Thorndike's Law of Effect):** Positive outcomes repeat — but the type of positive outcome matters (emotional vs transactional)
- Frame as: **The brands winning at loyalty marketing aren't offering bigger discounts — they're triggering deeper psychological mechanisms**

### Frameworks to reference
- **"Do/Get" vs "Feel/Do"** (Salesforce) — transactional vs emotional loyalty
- **Loyalty Ladder** — Lead → Customer → Loyal Customer → Advocate (LoyaltyLion)
- **RFM Model** — Recency, Frequency, Monetary value for segmentation
- **Redemption Elasticity** — sweet spot between attainable and aspirational rewards (Influence.io)

### Brand examples strategy
- Use **1–2 enterprise examples** for recognition (Starbucks, REI) — keep brief
- Focus on **mid-size brand examples** at ICP scale ($50K–$500K/mo Shopify stores)
- Avoid citing competitor platform case studies (Growave's Doodle Dogs, etc.)
