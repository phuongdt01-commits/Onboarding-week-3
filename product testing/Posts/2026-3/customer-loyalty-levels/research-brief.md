## Core 6 Factors + Evidence

### 1. **Shopify Loyalty Levels Framework**
**What customers need to understand:** The progression from browsers to advocates, and the behavioral/revenue characteristics of each level.

**Evidence:**
- **Pareto Principle (verified):** 20% of customers generate 80% of revenue (Bain & Company, Shopify confirmed)
  - Implication: Moving customers from bottom 80% → top 20% is highest ROI activity
- **Case Study – MoxieLash:** 3x increase in second purchase rate (LoyaltyLion case study)
  - Shows clear tier transition: First Buyer → Repeat Buyer (measurable)
- **Case Study – Ofra Cosmetics:** 56% increase in repeat purchase rates (LoyaltyLion case study)
  - Demonstrates level-up effect: single buyer → repeat buyer cohort

**Joy's Angle:** Define 4–5 levels specific to e-commerce (Browse → First Buy → Repeat Buyer → VIP/Advocate → Champion). Show revenue impact per tier.

---

### 2. **Practical Tactics to Move Customers UP Levels**
**What customers need:** Specific, actionable triggers/programs to move customers from one level to the next.

**Evidence:**
- **MoxieLash:** 1.5x higher average spend among loyalty program members vs. non-members
  - Tactic: Program enrollment triggers first-tier uplift
- **Ofra Cosmetics:** 59% increase in customer spend (attributed to tiered rewards/referral program)
  - Tactic: Referral incentives move repeats → advocates
- **Pulse Boutique:** 39% uplift in returning customers via VIP tier program
  - Tactic: Exclusivity/tiering drives repeat behavior

**Joy's Angle:** Walk through tactics per level—what triggers move Browse → First Buy, what moves First Buy → Repeat, etc. Use Assisted Orders as the north-star metric (referral link activations, discount code usage).

---

### 3. **Revenue Impact (Assisted Orders, AOV, CLV per Level)**
**What customers need:** Quantified ROI—how much does each level contribute to bottom line?

**Evidence:**
- **140% more spending by satisfied customers** (Harvard Business Review, August 2014)
  - Implication: High-level loyalty (emotional/behavioral) = 2.4x customer value vs. low-level
- **Acquisition cost multiplier: 5–25x** (Harvard Business Review, 2014)
  - Context: E-commerce typically 5–10x range
  - Implication: Every customer retained = saving $X in acquisition
- **Case Studies show AOV uplift:**
  - Pulse Boutique: 19% AOV increase (VIP tier)
  - Ofra Cosmetics: 59% spend increase (referral/tier program)
  - MoxieLash: 1.5x higher AOV (loyalty members)

**Joy's Angle:** Frame as "Assisted Orders" = revenue directly from loyalty. Show CLV calculation: what does a Repeat Buyer vs. Advocate customer contribute to annual revenue? Link tier progression → revenue growth.

---

### 4. **Identification & Measurement (How to Tag/Identify Customer Levels)**
**What customers need:** A practical way to know which customer is at which level, and track transitions.

**Evidence:**
- **NPS Framework (verified from SERP):** Detractors (level 1), Passives (level 2), Promoters (level 3)
  - Standard for B2B, but e-commerce needs purchase-behavior segmentation instead
- **Pareto/RFM Segmentation (verified):** Recency, Frequency, Monetary value
  - Foundation for identifying which tier each customer occupies
- **Absence of Signal** (churn indicator): No engagement in 60–90 days = drop to lower tier
  - Measurement: Track engagement to identify at-risk customers

**Joy's Angle:** Show how Joy identifies customers per level using **purchase behavior data** (Recency, Frequency, AOV) + **engagement signals** (email opens, loyalty program activity). Provide a **simple segmentation table** merchants can use.

---

### 5. **Tiered Rewards & Gamification (E-commerce Specific)**
**What customers need:** Concrete reward structures that feel achievable but motivating.

**Evidence:**
- **Ofra Cosmetics Referral Program:** 56% increase repeat purchases + 59% spend increase + $180K referral revenue
  - Structure: Tiered referral bonuses (higher spend = more referral credits)
- **Pulse Boutique:** VIP tier program drove 39% returning customer uplift + 19% AOV increase
  - Structure: Exclusive perks (early access, birthday discount, free shipping threshold)
- **MoxieLash:** Program membership = 3x second purchase rate, 1.5x AOV
  - Structure: Points-based rewards (purchase points = future discount)

**Joy's Angle:** Show 3–4 gamification models (referral, points, VIP tiers, milestone bonuses) with real e-commerce outcomes. Emphasize **low-friction implementation** on Shopify.

---

### 6. **Recovery After Mistakes (Service Recovery Keeps Them at Higher Levels)**
**What customers need:** How to prevent downgrade when something goes wrong, and recover lost customers.

**Evidence:**
- **140% spend uplift by satisfied customers** (HBR 2014) implies inverse: poor experience = spending drop
- **"Closing the loop" on feedback:** Boosts retention up to 8.5%, increases NPS (CustomerGauge research)
  - Implication: Acknowledging mistakes + fast response = loyalty maintenance
- **SERP gap identified:** No competitor explains "recovery tactics" in loyalty context
  - Opportunity: Joy can own this angle

**Joy's Angle:** Show **recovery playbook**—what to do when a customer has a poor experience. Tie to loyalty levels: "A VIP customer with unresolved issue drops to Repeat Buyer tier unless recovered. Here's how to keep them elevated."
