## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent
Commercial-informational. Users want both:
- Tactical frameworks for implementing a pub loyalty scheme
- Strategic insights to drive repeat visits through technology and data

### Secondary intents
- Comparing mechanics: visit-based stamps vs. spend-based points vs. tiered memberships
- Identifying frictionless sign-up methods
- Understanding ROI of gamification (challenges, badges, race games)
- Optimising in-venue promotion (staff scripts, QR code placement)

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems
- **Market saturation:** Crowded hospitality market — must differentiate through experience
- **Operational friction:** Program must not slow down busy service shifts
- **Data blindness:** Physical stamp cards give no insight into basket size or visit times
- **Margin protection:** Rewards must feel generous to customers but protect pub margins
- **Sign-up barriers:** Customer reluctance to download new apps

### What this article MUST achieve
1. Serve both independent pub owners AND small hospitality groups
2. Address legal gaps competitors miss (alcohol regulations on free drink rewards, GDPR)
3. Be platform-agnostic — not biased toward any single tool
4. Bridge the physical → digital loyalty transition practically
5. Position Joy for pubs that have an online presence (merchandise, gift cards, events on Shopify)

- Open Loyalty — API-first loyalty engine
- Quickpad / Stamper app — hardware mentioned in competitor content

### Joy integration angle
Joy is a Shopify loyalty app. Pubs are not typically Shopify merchants. Bridge:
- Pubs with online shops (merchandise, branded gifts, event tickets, gift cards on Shopify)
- Use Joy as an example of what a modern loyalty platform can do (data, triggers, analytics)
- Position Joy in a "if you also sell online" or "for hospitality brands going D2C" framing
- Do NOT force Joy into every section — 1-2 natural mentions only

### Legal/compliance gaps to address
- Alcohol regulations: Promoting "free drinks" as rewards may be legally restricted in UK/EU
- GDPR: Collecting customer data via loyalty sign-up requires consent, clear privacy policy
- These gaps are completely absent from competitor content — owning them builds trust
