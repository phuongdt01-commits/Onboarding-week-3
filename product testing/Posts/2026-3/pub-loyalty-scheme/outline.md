## **SECTION 1: What Is a Pub Loyalty Scheme? — The 3 Models Every Operator Should Know**
*(Word count: 350–400 words)*

**Section Overview:**
Defines what a pub loyalty scheme actually is and maps the three core mechanics — visit-based stamps, spend-based points, and tiered memberships. Each model gets a clear "best for" use case so pub operators can self-select. This is the foundational section that the rest of the article builds on.

---

### **H3.1: Visit-Based Stamps — The Simplest Entry Point**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- What it is: Customer gets a stamp per visit (or per qualifying purchase). After X stamps, they earn a reward (free drink, food item, or discount).
- How it works: Physical card or digital wallet stamp (Apple/Google Wallet — no app download needed). Customer scans QR code at bar, staff validates.
- Best for: Independent pubs with simple menus, high walk-in traffic, and operators who want zero complexity.
- Advantage: Lowest friction sign-up. Customer doesn't need to create an account or download anything.
- Limitation: No data on what customers order, how much they spend, or when they visit. You know they came — that's it.

**Key claim to make:** Stamp cards get customers through the door again, but they give you zero intelligence about who those customers are or what they want.

**Needs clarification:** None — general mechanics, no specific stat needed.

---

### **H3.2: Spend-Based Points — Data-Rich and Margin-Friendly**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- What it is: Customer earns points per pound/dollar spent (e.g., 1 point per £1). Points accumulate toward rewards with defined redemption thresholds.
- How it works: Tied to POS or digital ordering system. Customer identifies via phone number, app, or card tap. Every transaction tracked.
- Best for: Pubs with food menus, cocktail bars, or venues where average spend varies significantly between customers.
- Advantage: You see basket size, visit frequency, peak hours, and top-spending regulars. Rewards scale with spend — protects margins because high-spenders earn more, but they also spend more.
- Limitation: Requires POS integration or digital ordering. More setup than stamps.

**Key claim to make:** Points programs turn every transaction into a data point — you stop guessing who your best customers are and start knowing.

**Needs clarification:** None.

---

### **H3.3: Tiered Memberships — For Pubs With a Premium Play**
*(~80–100 words)*

**Content to write:**
- What it is: Customers progress through levels (e.g., Regular → Local → VIP) based on visit count or total spend. Higher tiers unlock better perks.
- Best for: Gastropubs, brewery taprooms, or venue groups with premium experiences (private events, tasting menus, first access to new releases).
- Advantage: Creates aspiration — customers see the next tier and visit more often to reach it. VIP tier drives word-of-mouth.
- Limitation: Requires enough differentiated perks to justify tiers. A pub that serves only drinks may struggle to create meaningful tier gaps.

**Key claim to make:** Tiers work when you have genuinely different experiences to unlock — if every tier gets "10% off," the structure is cosmetic.

**Needs clarification:** None.

## **SECTION 2: Pub Loyalty Card vs. Digital — Why Paper Stamp Cards Are Holding You Back**
*(Word count: 300–350 words)*

**Section Overview:**

---

### **H3.1: The Pub Stamp Card Problem — Lost Cards, Zero Data, Easy Fraud**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Physical stamp cards: customers lose them, staff forget to stamp, friends stamp each other's cards (fraud). No data captured — you can't email a lost stamp card holder.
- Data blindness: Paper cards don't tell you visit frequency, peak times, average spend, or which customers churned. You're running a reward program with no feedback loop.
- Operational friction: During busy Friday/Saturday shifts, asking staff to find, stamp, and return a physical card slows service. In basement bars with poor Wi-Fi, even some digital solutions fail.
- The cost of lost cards: Every lost card is a broken loyalty loop — the customer was 7/10 stamps toward a reward and now starts over (or doesn't).

**Key claim to make:** Paper stamp cards reward visits but punish both the customer (lost progress) and the pub (lost data) — they're a 1990s tool in a 2026 market.

**Needs clarification:** Do we have stats on stamp card loss rates or fraud rates? If not, use general framing.

---

### **H3.2: Digital Pub Loyalty Card — Mobile Wallet, No App Download**
*(~120–140 words)*

**Content to write:**
- The solution: Digital stamp cards via Apple Wallet / Google Wallet. Customer scans a QR code at the bar → card saves to their phone wallet. No app download, no account creation, no friction.
- How it works: Each visit or qualifying purchase triggers a digital stamp via NFC tap or QR scan. Customer sees stamp progress on their lock screen.
- Data captured: Visit date/time, location (for multi-site pubs), stamp count, redemption history. Pub owner gets a dashboard with customer analytics.
- Offline capability: Mobile wallet cards work without Wi-Fi after initial save — critical for basement bars, beer gardens, and venues with spotty connectivity.
- Push notifications: Send "You're 2 stamps away from a free pint" directly to the customer's lock screen — no email open rate dependency.

**Key claim to make:** A digital pub loyalty card gives you the simplicity of stamps with the intelligence of a CRM — and the customer never downloads anything.

**Needs clarification:** None — general digital wallet mechanics.

## **SECTION 3: How to Design a Bar Loyalty Program That Protects Margins**
*(Word count: 350–400 words)*

**Section Overview:**
The practical reward design section. Addresses the core fear every pub owner has: "Won't loyalty rewards eat my margins?" Shows how to structure rewards that feel generous to customers but cost the pub less than they appear. Covers reward types, earn/burn ratios, and expiry mechanics.

---

### **H3.1: Reward Types That Work for Pubs — Beyond Free Pints**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Free drink rewards are the default — but they're not always the best margin play. A free pint costs the pub £1.50–£2.50 in COGS but feels like a £5–£6 reward to the customer. That's a good ratio.
- Better margin options: Priority seating on busy nights (costs nothing), first access to new cask ales or guest beers (costs nothing), pub quiz team name on the VIP board (costs nothing), birthday pint (one per year — predictable cost).
- Experience rewards: Brewery tour tickets, meet-the-brewer nights, private tasting events — these create memorable moments that drive word-of-mouth and cost the pub primarily in staff time, not product.
- Tiered reward menu: Let members choose between low-cost (free coffee) and aspirational (private event invite) rewards.

**Key claim to make:** The best pub loyalty rewards cost the operator almost nothing but feel exclusive to the customer — priority access, experiences, and recognition outperform free drinks.

**Needs clarification:** Can we verify average UK pub COGS for a pint? General industry range (£1.50–£2.50) is widely cited but worth confirming.

---

### **H3.2: Earn/Burn Ratio — How to Set Points Without Bleeding Profit**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- The earn/burn ratio determines your actual loyalty cost. Example: 1 point per £1 spent, 100 points = £5 reward. That's a 5% reward rate — sustainable for most pubs.
- Rule of thumb: Keep your effective reward rate between 3–7% of customer spend. Below 3%, customers don't feel rewarded. Above 7%, you're eroding margins.
- Expiry mechanics: Points that never expire create a growing liability on your books. Set a 6–12 month rolling expiry with a reminder notification 30 days before expiry. This creates urgency (visit now or lose points) and caps your cost exposure.
- Breakage: Not every customer redeems. Industry average breakage (unredeemed points) is 20–30%. Factor this into your cost model — your actual reward cost is lower than the stated rate.

**Key claim to make:** A 5% reward rate with 12-month expiry and natural breakage means your pub loyalty scheme costs closer to 3.5–4% of revenue — less than most marketing spend.

**Needs clarification:** Breakage rates (20–30%) are widely cited in loyalty industry reports — do we have a primary source (e.g., Bond Brand Loyalty Report)?

---

### **H3.3: Fraud Prevention — Stopping Stamp Abuse and Point Gaming**
*(~80–100 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Common pub loyalty fraud: friends stamping each other's cards, staff giving stamps without purchases, customers claiming they "forgot" their card and getting double stamps.
- Digital prevention: Unique QR codes per transaction, staff PIN verification, GPS-validated check-ins, time-gating (one stamp per visit, minimum 2-hour gap between stamps).
- Point gaming: Customers splitting one large order into multiple small transactions to earn more points. Fix: Set minimum spend threshold per point-earning transaction.
- Staff accountability: Digital systems log which staff member validated each stamp/point — creates an audit trail.

**Key claim to make:** Digital loyalty systems don't just prevent fraud — they create accountability for both customers and staff that paper cards never could.

**Needs clarification:** None — general fraud prevention mechanics.

## **SECTION 4: Pub Loyalty Scheme Legal Pitfalls — Alcohol Regulations and GDPR**
*(Word count: 250–300 words)*

**Section Overview:**
The section no competitor covers. Addresses two critical legal gaps: (1) alcohol reward regulations — offering "free drinks" as loyalty rewards may breach licensing laws in the UK/EU, and (2) GDPR compliance for collecting customer data through loyalty sign-ups. This is our primary information gain angle.

---

### **H3.1: Alcohol Reward Regulations — Can You Give Away Free Drinks?**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- UK Licensing Act 2003: Irresponsible drink promotions are prohibited. The Mandatory Licensing Conditions (2014) specifically ban promotions that encourage excessive drinking.
- The grey area: A "buy 9 pints, get 1 free" stamp card could be interpreted as encouraging volume consumption. The risk increases if the reward is specifically an alcoholic drink with no alternative option.
- Safer approach: Offer choice — "Redeem for a free pint OR a free soft drink OR a £5 food voucher." This demonstrates responsible promotion and protects your licence.
- Multi-option rewards also broaden appeal: designated drivers, non-drinkers, and lunch-only visitors all find value.
- Scotland: Additional regulations under the Alcohol (Minimum Pricing) (Scotland) Act 2012 — free alcohol promotions face stricter scrutiny.

**Key claim to make:** Offering exclusively alcoholic rewards in a pub loyalty scheme is a licensing risk — always include non-alcoholic or food alternatives to stay compliant and broaden appeal.

**Needs clarification:** Can we verify specific Mandatory Licensing Conditions clauses relevant to loyalty rewards? General reference to the 2003 Act and 2014 Conditions is sufficient, but exact clause numbers would strengthen credibility.

---

### **H3.2: GDPR Compliance — What Pub Operators Must Do When Collecting Customer Data**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Any digital loyalty scheme collects personal data: name, email, phone number, visit history, spend data. Under GDPR (UK GDPR post-Brexit), this requires explicit consent.
- What you must have: A clear privacy policy (what data you collect, why, how long you keep it, who sees it). Consent at sign-up (opt-in checkbox, not pre-ticked). Right to erasure (customer can request deletion).
- What most pubs miss: Using loyalty data for marketing (emails, SMS) requires separate marketing consent — signing up for the loyalty scheme does NOT automatically opt customers into marketing communications.
- Data retention: Don't keep customer data indefinitely. Set a retention period (e.g., 24 months of inactivity → data deleted).
- Practical step: Add a one-paragraph privacy notice at the QR code sign-up point.

**Key claim to make:** GDPR compliance for a pub loyalty scheme isn't complex — it's a privacy notice, an opt-in checkbox, and a data retention policy. But most pubs skip it entirely, creating unnecessary risk.

**Needs clarification:** None — standard GDPR requirements, well-documented.

## **SECTION 5: Real Hospitality Loyalty Scheme Examples — What Works in Practice**

### **H3.1: The Independent Pub — Simple Stamp Card Done Right**
*(~80–100 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Profile: A single-location pub running a digital stamp card via mobile wallet. 10 stamps = free drink or £5 food credit (multi-option reward for licensing compliance).
- What works: QR code on every table + one at the bar. Staff trained with a one-line script: "Scan the QR to join our loyalty — you're halfway to a free round." No app download, 15-second sign-up.
- Result: Regulars self-identify through the scheme. Pub owner sees visit frequency data for the first time. Top 20% of members visit 3x per week.
- Lesson: Start simple. A stamp card with one clear reward and zero friction sign-up outperforms a complicated points system that nobody understands.

**Key claim to make:** The best independent pub loyalty scheme is the one customers actually use — and that means zero friction, one clear reward, and staff who promote it every shift.

### **H3.2: The Brewery Taproom — Points + Exclusive Access**
*(~80–100 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Profile: A craft brewery with 3 taproom locations running a points-based program. 1 point per £1 spent. 100 points = £5 off, 250 points = brewery tour invite, 500 points = "Founder's Club" membership (annual tasting event + name on taproom wall).
- What works: Points sync across all 3 locations — a customer who visits taproom A earns points redeemable at taproom B. Multi-site syncing eliminates "this card only works here" frustration.
- Aspiration layer: The "Founder's Club" tier at 500 points creates a visible goal. Members see other names on the wall and want to join.
- Lesson: When you have multiple locations, unified loyalty data across sites is non-negotiable.

**Key claim to make:** Multi-site pubs and brewery groups need loyalty data that syncs across locations — customers don't think in terms of "which branch," they think in terms of "my local."

## **SECTION 6: How to Launch Your Pub Loyalty Scheme — A 4-Week Rollout Plan**
*(Word count: 300–350 words)*

**Section Overview:**
Tactical launch plan for pub operators ready to act. Breaks the rollout into 4 weekly phases — from choosing a model to staff training to promotion to optimisation. Designed so a pub owner can screenshot this section and use it as a literal action plan.

---

### **H3.1: Week 1–2 — Choose Your Model and Set Up**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Decision: Stamps, points, or tiers? Use the Section 1 framework. Most independent pubs should start with digital stamps. Multi-site or gastropubs with online stores should consider points or tiers.
- Setup checklist: Choose a loyalty platform → Configure earning rules (stamps per visit OR points per £1) → Set reward(s) with multi-option choice (for licensing compliance) → Design the QR code sign-up flow → Write a one-paragraph privacy notice.
- Staff training: Every staff member should be able to explain the scheme in one sentence and prompt customers to join. Example script: "We've just launched our loyalty — scan the QR on your table, and your 10th visit earns you a free drink or food credit."
- Test internally: Run the scheme with staff for 3–5 days before public launch. Identify friction points.

**Key claim to make:** A pub loyalty scheme should take days to set up, not weeks — if your platform requires more than a week of configuration, it's too complex for hospitality.

**Needs clarification:** None — general launch framework.

---

### **H3.2: Week 3–4 — Launch, Promote, and Optimise**
*(~100–120 words)*

**Content to write:**
- Launch day: QR codes on every table, bar top, and receipt. A-frame sign at entrance. Social media announcement with "Join today and get your first stamp free" incentive.
- Staff activation: The #1 promotion channel for a pub loyalty scheme is staff. Every transaction is a prompt opportunity. Incentivise staff: "Team member who signs up the most members this month wins [prize]."
- Week 3 check: Review sign-up rate, stamp/point activity, and any customer complaints. Common issue: QR code too small or poorly placed. Fix: Print larger, place at eye level at the bar.
- Week 4 optimise: Look at redemption rate. If nobody is redeeming, the reward feels too far away — lower the threshold. If everyone redeems immediately, the threshold is too low — raise it or add a higher-tier reward.

**Key claim to make:** The first month of a pub loyalty scheme is about learning, not perfecting — launch fast, measure everything, and adjust based on real customer behaviour.
