## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent

**Primary:** Commercial investigation — merchants evaluating whether loyalty discounts work and which type delivers best ROI.

**Secondary:**
- Educational: Understanding *why* loyalty discounts drive retention (psychology of reciprocity, status, gamification)
- Strategic/How-to: How to set the right discount value and structure without eroding margins
- Problem-aware: Merchants worried about discount dependency and margin damage

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems

Shopify store owners doing $50K–$500K/month who are:
- Considering adding discounts to a loyalty program but worried about training customers to wait for deals
- Unsure how to set reward values (too high = margin erosion; too low = no behavior change)
- Looking for proof that loyalty discounts work differently from blanket promotional discounts
- Concerned about program abuse and discount stacking

### What this article MUST achieve

1. Distinguish loyalty discounts from promotional discounts (loyalty discounts are earned, not given — this is the psychological and business-model difference)
2. Give merchants a framework to set discount value without destroying margins
3. Show which types of loyalty discounts work for which business contexts
4. Flag the risks (discount dependency, margin erosion) AND how to prevent them
5. Drive toward Joy's loyalty features (points, VIP tiers, referral rewards) as the implementation vehicle

## H/ Notes for Writer

**Core strategic angle:** Loyalty discounts ≠ promotional discounts. The difference is *earned value vs. given value*. Promotional discounts reduce price for everyone, training customers to wait for sales. Loyalty discounts are earned through behavior, creating a reciprocal relationship where the discount rewards past action rather than incentivizing present inaction.

**Joy features to feature:**
- Points redemption → discount codes (the most common loyalty discount mechanic)
- VIP tier unlock discounts (tier-based)
- Referral discount codes (referral mechanic)
- Birthday/anniversary discount rewards (time-based)
- Flash rewards for VIP members (surprise & delight)

**Brand examples to use (with publicly available data):**
- **Starbucks Rewards** — stars redeemable for free items, not blanket discounts; keeps ASP high
- **Sephora Beauty Insider** — tiered access rather than percentage discounts at lower tiers
- **Amazon Prime** — paid tier that justifies discount access through membership fee (margins protected)

**The margin math framework (core unique section):**
Reward value benchmark from Yotpo: 1–5% of spend.
At 2% reward value: for a $100 order, the customer earns $2.
If that customer comes back for a second purchase because of the reward, CAC for order #2 = $2 (vs. $20–50 typical paid CAC).
The discount pays for itself at second purchase — this is the ROI argument.

**Key terms to define:**
- Loyalty discount (vs. promotional discount)
- Reward value / redemption value
- Discount dependency
- Earned discount vs. given discount
- Breakeven repeat purchase

**Tone reminder:** Strategic + Motivational. This is a merchant who is skeptical about discounts hurting margins. Lead with their fear, flip it with the earned-vs-given distinction, then arm them with the math.

> **REMINDER:** Stats must come from primary sources only (brand earnings, academic research, McKinsey/Bain/Harvard, government data). Do NOT cite competitor platform blogs (Antavo, Yotpo, LoyaltyLion, Smile.io, Open Loyalty, etc.) as sources for data.
