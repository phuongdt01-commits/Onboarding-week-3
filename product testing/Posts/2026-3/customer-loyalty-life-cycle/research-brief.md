## G/ Notes for Writer

### Proposed 5-stage lifecycle framework (Joy's version)
Use a unified 5-stage model. Give it a Joy flavor:

1. **Awareness** — First contact. Customer discovers your brand.
2. **First Purchase** — Conversion. The first transaction and post-purchase experience.
3. **Engagement** — Repeat interactions: emails opened, points earned, reviews left.
4. **Retention** — Habitual buying. Customer shows a repeat purchase pattern.
5. **Advocacy** — Active promotion. Customer refers others, leaves reviews, shares content.

> **Note for writer:** Distinguish "customer lifecycle" (generic marketing funnel) from "customer **loyalty** lifecycle" — the loyalty version begins AFTER first purchase. The awareness/acquisition stage is the entry point, but the loyalty journey is about what happens from first purchase onward. This is a key framing most competitors miss.

### Stage-specific KPIs
- Awareness → Cost per acquisition (CPA), traffic sources
- First Purchase → Conversion rate, cart abandonment rate, post-purchase email open rate
- Engagement → Email open/click rates, loyalty program enrollment rate, points earned per member
- Retention → Repeat purchase rate, purchase frequency, churn rate, customer retention rate
- Advocacy → NPS score, referral conversion rate, review submission rate, UGC volume

### Joy feature placement
- **Engagement stage:** Joy's points-earning mechanics (for every purchase, review, birthday, etc.)
- **Retention stage:** Joy's tiered VIP program — customers level up as they buy more, creating a "status" incentive to stay
- **Advocacy stage:** Joy's referral program — turns satisfied loyalists into active referrers with tracked referral links

All stats below have been fact-checked against primary sources.

**STAT 1 ✅ — Retention is cheaper than acquisition**
- **Confirmed claim:** "Acquiring a new customer is anywhere from 5 to 25 times more expensive than retaining an existing one"
- **Source:** Amy Gallo, Harvard Business Review, Oct 2014 — citing research by Frederick Reichheld of Bain & Company
- **URL:** https://hbr.org/2014/10/the-value-of-keeping-the-right-customers
- **Use at:** Retention stage intro — why this stage matters most

---

**STAT 2 ✅ — Retention impact on profit**
- **Confirmed claim:** "Increasing customer retention rates by 5% increases profits by 25% to 95%"
- **Source:** Same HBR article above, directly attributed to Frederick Reichheld of Bain & Company
- **URL:** https://hbr.org/2014/10/the-value-of-keeping-the-right-customers
- **Use at:** Retention stage — the business case for investing in retention

---

**STAT 3 ✅ — Repeat customers spend exponentially more over time**
- **Confirmed claim:** "In apparel e-tailing, repeat customers spend more than twice as much in months 24–30 of their relationships than they do in the first six months"
- **Source:** Harvard Business Review, "E-Loyalty: Your Secret Weapon on the Web," 2000
- **URL:** https://hbr.org/2000/07/e-loyalty-your-secret-weapon-on-the-web
- **Use at:** Retention/Advocacy stage — shows why long-term customers are worth cultivating
- **Note:** E-commerce specific, apparel industry — relevant to Shopify merchants

---

**STAT 4 ✅ — One bad experience ends customer relationships**
- **Confirmed claim:** "32% of all customers would stop doing business with a brand they loved after just one bad experience"
- **Confirmed claim:** "59% will walk away after several bad experiences; 17% after just one bad experience" (US consumers)
- **Source:** PwC, "Future of Customer Experience Survey," 2022
- **URL:** https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/consulting/library/consumer-intelligence-series/future-of-customer-experience.html
- **Use at:** First Purchase / Engagement stage — why friction and poor CX destroy lifecycle momentum

---

**STAT 5 ✅ — Great experiences command premium pricing**
- **Confirmed claim:** Customers who feel valued will pay "up to a 16% price premium on products and services, plus increased loyalty"
- **Source:** PwC, "Future of Customer Experience Survey," 2022
- **URL:** https://www.pwc.com/us/en/services/consulting/library/consumer-intelligence-series/future-of-customer-experience.html
- **Use at:** Engagement/Retention stage — experience investment pays off in price tolerance
