## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent

**Primary:** Informational + Commercial Investigation — business professionals building a case for customer loyalty investment. Seeking quantifiable advantages, categorized by business impact, to justify loyalty programs to stakeholders or guide strategic planning.

**Secondary intents:**
- Educational/Career Growth — professionals understanding loyalty as a business function
- Operational Planning — decision-makers evaluating budget/ROI before launching
- Technical Integration — marketing ops exploring data flow (CRM, CDP integration)

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems

1. **High Acquisition Costs** — CAC has risen 222% since 2013; merchants lose $29 per new customer acquired (SimplicityDX 2022). Brands need cheaper growth channels.
2. **Market Saturation** — Difficulty standing out in crowded markets where competitors run aggressive discounts.
3. **Data Gaps** — Guest shoppers provide no behavioral data; personalization impossible without first-party data infrastructure.
4. **Revenue Instability** — Unpredictable purchase cycles and "slow seasons" create cash flow volatility.
5. **High Churn** — Customers disappearing after 1–2 purchases; 77% of transactional-only loyalty programs fail within 2 years (Capgemini 2015).

### What this article MUST achieve

1. **Quantify** every advantage with verified primary-source stats (not generic claims)
2. **Categorize** benefits by business impact (revenue, data, brand, competitive) — not just a flat numbered list
3. **Address cost vs. ROI** head-on (the #1 objection for Solution Aware readers)
4. **Include failure points** — what kills programs (HBR/Bain 2024) — to build credibility and differentiate from competitor listicles that only show the upside
5. **Position Joy** as the platform that delivers these advantages for Shopify brands
