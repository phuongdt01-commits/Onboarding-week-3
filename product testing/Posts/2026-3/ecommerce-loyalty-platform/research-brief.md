## 8/ Notes for Writer

### Core Factors for Article Structure (7 Approved)

1. **Retention Economics & Business Case** — why loyalty platforms are a revenue investment
2. **Core Program Mechanics** — points, tiers, referrals, paid memberships, gamification
3. **Integration & Technical Architecture** — API-first, Shopify compatibility, headless, POS
4. **Personalization & AI-Driven Engagement** — tailored rewards, dynamic segmentation, predictive
5. **Omnichannel Capabilities** — unified web/mobile/in-store experience, mobile wallets
6. **Analytics & ROI Measurement** — dashboards, CLV tracking, revenue attribution, KPIs
7. **Scalability & Total Cost of Ownership** — pricing, hidden costs, implementation timelines

### Article Strategy
- **Format:** Strategic guide + soft comparison (buyer's guide + landscape overview blend)
- **Funnel Stage:** Solution Aware
- **Tone:** Strategic/Authoritative + Motivational (Joy brand voice)
- **Related concerns to weave in:** Hidden setup costs, implementation timelines, loyalty fatigue solutions, AI personalization depth

### Joy Positioning Opportunities
- **Assisted Orders** as the north-star metric (unique to Joy — competitors don't track this)
- **Shopify-native flexibility** — deep integration without the "enterprise-only" pricing
- **Point liability visibility** — built-in tracking that competitors charge extra for
- **Fast implementation** — 15-minute setup vs. weeks-long enterprise onboarding
- Position naturally through evaluation criteria, NOT as a product comparison entry

### Brand Examples to Reference (from competitor research)
- Sephora Beauty Insider (tiered program)
- Amazon Prime (paid membership model)
- Starbucks Rewards (gamification + mobile-first)

### Content Gaps to Fill (Differentiators)
1. **Hidden cost breakdown** — setup fees, integration costs, point liability accounting

3. **AI personalization beyond buzzwords** — what real AI-driven loyalty looks like vs. marketing claims
4. **Loyalty fatigue warning signs** — how to diagnose and fix a stagnant program
5. **Evaluation framework** — weighted criteria by business stage (startup vs. scaling vs. enterprise)
