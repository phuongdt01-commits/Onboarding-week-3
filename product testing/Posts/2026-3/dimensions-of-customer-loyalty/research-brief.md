**Terms needing clear definition in the article:**

- **Person-brand congruence** — when a brand's visible values match the customer's self-image; the behavioral science term for "this brand is me"
- **Structural bonds** — practical friction factors that make switching inconvenient (e.g., accumulated points, integrated apps, saved preferences)
- **Spurious loyalty** — repeat purchase driven by habit/convenience, not genuine preference (Dick & Basu 1994 term — customers who look loyal but will switch immediately when a better offer appears)

**Inconsistency to resolve:** "Promotion Dimension" and "Potential Dimension" are used interchangeably in the Ogilvy report. Use **Potential** as the canonical term in our article.

## G/ Notes for Writer

### The Core Framework (Ogilvy One — "The Four Dimensions of Loyalty")

Build the article around these four dimensions. Each section should: define the dimension → explain why it matters → show the mistake brands make → give 2–3 concrete ecommerce program mechanics.

| Dimension | Core idea | What it looks like in practice |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Principle** | Customers stay loyal when a brand's values visibly match their own | Public environmental commitments, cause-linked rewards, transparent sourcing, values-first messaging |
| **Potential** | Customers stay loyal when a brand actively helps them grow, improve, or achieve goals | Educational content rewards, skill-based challenges, personalized goal-tracking, AI-driven product journeys |
| **Culture** | Customers stay loyal when a brand reflects (or leads) the cultural identity they want to belong to | Aesthetic consistency, community rituals, brand voice, UGC that mirrors customer identity |
| **Community** | Customers stay loyal when they feel genuine belonging — to other customers, not just the brand | Referral programs that reward community-building, member forums, exclusive group events, peer recognition |

**Supporting framework — The Four Bonds** (complements the Four Dimensions):
- **Emotional bonds** — attachment, affection, brand love
- **Social bonds** — identity alignment, "my tribe"
- **Structural bonds** — switching friction (points, integrations, saved data)
- **Financial bonds** — discounts, perks, tangible value

The article can use the Four Dimensions as the main framework and the Four Bonds as the underlying mechanism that each dimension activates.

### Behavioral Science Layer (use selectively, don't overload)

- **Regulatory Focus Theory** (Higgins, 1997 — Columbia University): Customers with a *promotion* focus respond to aspiration rewards (gain-framed: "earn your way to Gold status"). Customers with a *prevention* focus respond to loss-avoidance framing ("don't lose your points"). Joy's tier expiry warnings and milestone nudges activate both.
- **Conscientiousness** trait: Highly conscientious customers respond well to structured, rule-based programs (clear earn/redeem rules, predictable tiers). Low-conscientiousness customers respond to spontaneous surprise rewards and gamification.
- **Person-brand congruence**: The psychological mechanism behind Principle loyalty. When a customer sees their own values in the brand's behavior, switching feels like a small betrayal of their identity — the strongest retention force available.
