## D/ Main Intent & User Context

### Search intent

**Primary: Informational-Educational (Problem Aware)**

Users search this keyword to understand *what* relationship marketing is, *how* it connects to customer loyalty, and *why* it matters — before seeking specific tools or solutions. They want a conceptual framework, not an implementation guide.

**Secondary intents:**
- ROI validation — justify shifting budget from acquisition to retention
- Benchmarking — compare relationship marketing approaches across industries
- Consumer psychology — understand trust, emotional connection, and commitment drivers

### User context (ICP) = Core user problems

- **High acquisition costs** — spending aggressively on new customers while existing ones churn
- **Transactional mindset** — running discounts and promotions without building lasting relationships
- **Commoditization pressure** — products/prices feel interchangeable; no emotional differentiation
- **Generic messaging** — broadcasting to everyone instead of personalizing to individuals
- **Lack of trust signals** — no systematic approach to building credibility with customers
- **No framework** — gut-feeling approach to "loyalty" without understanding the underlying constructs

### What this article MUST achieve

1. **Define** relationship marketing clearly and contrast it with transactional marketing
2. **Establish the framework** — the 4–5 core pillars (trust, commitment, communication, conflict handling, personalization) with evidence
3. **Make the business case** — retention economics, CLV, the cost of churn
4. **Connect to ecommerce reality** — translate academic concepts into what Shopify merchants experience daily
5. **Plant the seed** — Problem Aware readers should leave understanding *why* they need to shift from transactional to relational, without being sold a specific tool

- **Gummesson** — RM strategy where relationship management is the fundamental issue
