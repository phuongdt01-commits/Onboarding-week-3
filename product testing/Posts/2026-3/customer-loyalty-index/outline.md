### H3: Customer Loyalty Index: How the 3-Question Survey Works
**Keywords:** customer loyalty index, customer loyalty metrics
**Word Count:** 220–280 words

**Content Structure:**
- CLI is an average of three survey responses, each on a consistent scale (typically 1–6)
- **Question 1:** "How likely are you to recommend us to a friend?" (Advocacy/NPS component)
- **Question 2:** "How likely are you to repurchase from us?" (Behavioral intent)
- **Question 3:** "How likely are you to try other products from us?" (Cross-sell intent)
- **Formula:** CLI = (Q1 + Q2 + Q3) / 3
- **Example:** If customer scores 5, 4, 6 → CLI = 5.0

**Key Claims:**
- 3-question model is industry standard for CLI
- 1–6 scale enforces directional choice (avoids neutral/fence-sitting)
- CLI produces single numerical score that predicts multiple outcomes (churn, revenue, CLV)

**Clarification Notes:**
- Explain why 1–6 scale (not 1–10): Forces decision, easier response, avoids midpoint hedging
- Show how each question maps to a loyalty outcome (advocacy → referrals, repeat → retention, cross-buy → expansion)
- Address survey fatigue: 3 questions is minimal; high response rates expected
