### Key Narrative Arc

1. **Open with scale + the democratic paradox** — 170M members, zero tiers. The world's largest loyalty program is also its simplest. Why does IKEA reject the tiered model that virtually every competitor uses? Because their brand promise ("for the many people") makes hierarchy impossible — but that choice has real tradeoffs.
2. **The 73-year evolution** — From Silverklubben (1952) to IKEA Family (1984) to the 2025 points refresh. Brief timeline showing how the program evolved from a simple customer club to a global engagement platform. Not a history lesson — a case study in loyalty program evolution.
3. **The 2025 pivot: from perks to points** — Why IKEA added points after 40 years of perks-only. The catalyst: discontinuing the popular 5% in-store discount (Feb 2024) created a perceived value gap. Points were the response — but calibrated differently across currencies ($1 = 1pt US, £5 = 1pt UK). Break down the full earning/redemption structure.
4. **Engagement-first earning** — The strategic decision to reward wishlists, planning appointments, event attendance, and app logins alongside purchases. This isn't generosity — it's a data strategy. Every non-purchase interaction generates high-intent behavioral signals.
5. **The hub-and-spoke tech architecture** — How IKEA runs one loyalty program across 31 markets with regional partners, shared APIs, and localized content. The API-first approach enables rapid market launches. Break down what this means for brands scaling internationally.
6. **Global-local calibration challenges** — $1/point in the US vs £5/point in the UK. Country-specific cards. Regional benefit variations. The operational complexity of "democratic" loyalty at global scale.
7. **Emotional loyalty mechanics** — Free coffee as a micro-moment. DIY workshops as knowledge transfer. Oops insurance as problem-solving. Life event integration (gift registries). How IKEA stays relevant between big furniture purchases.
8. **The omnichannel honesty report** — Coupons can't be used online. Loyalty account is separate from shopping account. Regional inconsistencies in benefits. Generic welcome emails. Document these gaps transparently — it adds credibility and teaches what to avoid.
9. **Translate to Shopify scale** — Every section ends with "here's how ambitious brands do this with Joy."
10. **Close with the strategic lesson** — IKEA proves that loyalty programs don't need tiers to drive 50%+ of revenue. But "democratic" at 170M members requires technology, operational discipline, and the courage to prioritize engagement over transaction. The question for Shopify merchants: do you want a smaller, committed community or a massive, flat membership? Joy supports both — the strategic choice is yours.
