## Section 3: What Is a Loyalty Loop? (Concept Definition)
**Word Count:** 350–400 words

**Copy Angle:**
Define the loyalty loop as a continuous, circular customer relationship model (not linear funnel). Show the 4–5 stages and why circular > linear. Introduce psychological principle (emotional connection, anchoring). Use plain language; avoid jargon.

**Key Points:**
- **Loop definition:** Circular journey: Purchase → Experience → Advocacy → Repeat Purchase → Loop back
- **vs. traditional funnel:** Linear (Awareness → Consideration → Purchase → Exit). Funnel stops at purchase; loop accelerates from there.
- **Why circular works:** Customers become advocates, refer friends, self-reinforce. No single "exit" point.
- **Psychological hook:** Anchoring bias (first experience locks in loyalty), emotional connection (unexpected "wows" create loyalty spirals)

**Stats to Use:**

## Section 4: The 4 Stages of the Loyalty Loop
**Word Count:** 450–550 words

**Copy Angle:**
Walk through each loop stage with Shopify store examples. Show how post-purchase experience, behavioral engagement, and lapsed recovery all feed the loop. Make it tactical—merchants should see their own store in these examples.

**Stages:**

**Stage 1: Post-Purchase Experience (The First 90 Days)**
- Deliver "wow" after the sale (not before)
- Handwritten note, surprise bonus points, instant discount code for next purchase
- Data: 4.5% higher repeat rate, 94% value quick resolution (Accenture)
- Example: Cosmetics store sends "Welcome to the loop" email with 10% referral bonus within hours of purchase

**Stage 2: Behavioral Engagement (The Active Loop)**
- Reward actions, not just transactions (referrals, reviews, repeat buys, milestones)
- Move from "spend $100 = 100 points" to "refer 3 friends = exclusive tier" or "4th purchase = double points"
- Keeps customers actively participating in their own loyalty
- Example: Apparel brand unlocks "Champion" tier when customer hits 3 referrals + 5 purchases

**Stage 3: Advocacy & Referral (Closing the External Loop)**
- Happy customers refer ~11 people on average (Amex 2017)
- Incentivize referral links, reviews, user-generated content
- Turn customers into brand ambassadors
- Example: Beauty subscription tracks Assisted Orders from referral links; rewards both referrer and referee

**Stage 4: Lapsed Customer Recovery (Closing the Internal Loop)**
- 50%+ subscription/purchase churn is normal
- Identify churn signals (no purchase in 60 days, referral inactive, engagement dropped)
- Segment high-value lapsed vs. low-value; customize win-back offers
- Example: VIP customer hasn't bought in 45 days → targeted email: "We miss you. Here's 20% off + free shipping"

## Section 6: Lapsed Customer Recovery — Your Biggest Retention Opportunity
**Word Count:** 300–350 words

**Copy Angle:**
Lapsed customers are low-hanging fruit. They've bought once, proven purchase intent, but stopped. Win-back is ROI-positive (retention costs less than acquisition). Show segmentation logic + win-back sequences.

**Key Points:**
- **Churn signals:** No purchase in 30/60/90 days, referral inactive, engagement zero
- **Segmentation matters:**
  - VIP lapsed (5+ lifetime purchases, high AOV) → premium win-back (exclusive discount, personal note)
  - Standard lapsed (1–2 purchases) → standard win-back (basic discount, reminder email)
  - Low-value lapsed (impulse buyer, single purchase) → low-touch or archive
- **Win-back sequences:** 3-touch automated flow
  - Touch 1: Soft email (we miss you) + mild incentive (10% off)
  - Touch 2: SMS or push (48–72 hrs later) with urgency (expires in 5 days)
  - Touch 3: Final email (last chance) with stronger incentive (20% off or free gift)
- **ROI math:** Winning back 1 lapsed customer (5x cheaper than CAC) = massive CLV lift

**Stats to Use:**
- 54% inactive = massive lapsed pool
- 5x cost rule = retention is cheaper
- 50%+ subscription churn = normal churn rate
