### Em-Dash Issue (Critical — Humanizer Task)

Em-dashes are banned in prose per brand rules. The rewritten article contains em-dashes throughout virtually every paragraph. Examples:

- "generated $30 billion in systemwide sales — up 50 percent year over year"
- "The 1,500-point tier delivers a Hash Brown — achievable in two visits"
- "fees that run 15 to 30 percent of order value — fees that run..."
- "one of the largest martech acquisitions in QSR history at that time"
- "The experience doesn't feel custom to most users — it's primarily offer segmentation"
- Multiple instances in each lesson and FAQ

**This is the Humanizer's primary structural task.** Replace each em-dash using the correct substitution:
- Elaboration → colon
- Flow/contrast → comma
- Hard break → period
- Connected clauses → semicolon
- Aside → parentheses

Exception: H2/H3 headings may retain em-dashes. None present in this article.
