### Visual Brief
Show the fragmentation problem → solution arc. Left side shows disconnected channels (website, store, mobile, email) with broken links or separate profiles. Right side or center shows those same channels unified/connected with a single customer profile connecting them all.

- 4–5 channel icons: Website, Physical Store, Mobile App, Email/SMS
- Visual metaphor: Broken/disconnected on left → unified/synced on right (or overlapping/integrated)
- Headline overlay: "Omnichannel Loyalty Programs: Best Programs by Industry (2026)"

- Color: Use Navy + Cerulean + Cherokee accent bar (asymmetric pill, ~9px height)

**Design approach:** Clean, icon-forward. Think Slack or Zapier style integration diagrams — simple icons with connecting lines.

## Image 5: Health Metrics Dashboard
**File:** `omnichannel-loyalty-metrics-05.webp` (1526×779px)
**Location:** FAQ section, after "How do you measure whether your omnichannel loyalty program is working?"
**Article context:** "The right metrics are Assisted Orders (how many purchases the loyalty program directly influenced), active member rate (healthy programs run 35–45% active), and redemption rate (healthy range: 20–35%)."

### Visual Brief
Three-metric dashboard showing healthy ranges and visual indicators.

**Metric 1: Assisted Orders**
- Label: "Assisted Orders"
- Visual: Large number or percentage with upward arrow
- Context: "Orders directly influenced by loyalty program"
- Indicator: Green (healthy signal)

**Metric 2: Active Member Rate**
- Label: "Active Member Rate"
- Visual: Percentage with gauge or bar
- Healthy range: 35–45%
- Indicator: Green if in range

**Metric 3: Redemption Rate**
- Label: "Redemption Rate"
- Visual: Percentage
- Healthy range: 20–35%
- Indicator: Green if in range
- Warning note: "Below 15% = engagement problem" (small red indicator)

**Design approach:**
- Three cards or three metric blocks arranged horizontally or in a grid
- Use Cerulean for healthy metrics, Bittersweet (red) only for the warning threshold
- All cards Frost glass (Variant C), Navy text
- Include small breakpoint callouts showing danger zones (e.g., "If below 20%, acquisition or threshold problem")
