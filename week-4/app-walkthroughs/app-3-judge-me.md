# App Walkthrough: Judge.me Product Reviews

## Basic Info
- **App name:** Judge.me Product Reviews
- **Category:** Product reviews
- **Pricing:** Free plan (unlimited reviews, photo/video, widgets, Google rich snippets, sync with Shop App/Etsy/Amazon, import from AliExpress). Awesome plan: $15/month (AI features, 130+ integrations, 16 widgets, Q&A, coupons & referrals, Google Shopping, Meta & TikTok sync, full customization). 15-day free trial on Awesome.
- **Rating:** 5.0 stars (37,003 reviews)
- **Install date:** March 30, 2026

## Installation Experience
- How long did installation take? ~2 minutes.
- Did the app request any permissions that seemed unusual? No. Access to orders, products, customers, and email. All expected for a review app.
- Was there an onboarding wizard? How helpful was it? Yes. Smooth flow: enable review widget → import existing reviews → set up email requests. Actionable, not theoretical.
- Did anything confuse you during setup? No major confusion. The number of widget options is large, so deciding which one to use takes some thought, but the setup itself is straightforward.

## First Impressions (written within 10 minutes of installing)
The dashboard opens with a review summary (total reviews, average rating), a setup checklist, and quick actions for importing, customizing widgets, and enabling emails.

About 70% intuitive. The UI isn't fancy, but it prioritizes action over design. Everything you need is where you'd expect it.

Core value is immediately clear without docs: "collect, display, and use reviews." No ambiguity about what this app does.

- What's the first thing you see in the dashboard? Review summary, setup checklist, quick actions.
- Does it feel intuitive or overwhelming? Mostly intuitive. Clean and functional.
- Can you figure out the core feature without reading documentation? Yes. Obvious from the first screen.

## Feature Testing

### 1. Review Collection (Email Requests)
- **Feature:** Automated Review Email Requests
- **What it claims to do:** Automatically collect reviews via email after order fulfillment
- **What it actually does:** Auto-sends email after order is fulfilled. Timing and content are customizable. Stable and reliable.
- **Setup difficulty:** Easy
- **Would a non-technical merchant figure this out?** Yes
- **Screenshots:** Email template editor, timing settings (e.g., 7 days after delivery)

### 2. Review Widget Display
- **Feature:** Review Widget Display
- **What it claims to do:** Display reviews on product pages
- **What it actually does:** Widget adds quickly (auto or manual embed). Multiple layouts available (carousel, list, star rating). Loads fast without slowing the page.
- **Setup difficulty:** Easy
- **Would a non-technical merchant figure this out?** Yes
- **Screenshots:** Widget customization panel, product page with reviews displayed

### 3. Photo & Video Reviews
- **Feature:** Photo & Video Reviews
- **What it claims to do:** Let customers upload media with their reviews
- **What it actually does:** Upload process is smooth. Media displays well in the UI. Visual reviews add noticeable trust.
- **Setup difficulty:** Easy
- **Would a non-technical merchant figure this out?** Yes
- **Screenshots:** Review with photo/video, gallery-style display

### 4. Review Import
- **Feature:** Review Import
- **What it claims to do:** Import reviews from AliExpress, CSV, and other sources
- **What it actually does:** Import is fast. Filtering by rating and content works well. Particularly useful for dropshipping stores that need reviews from day one.
- **Setup difficulty:** Easy
- **Would a non-technical merchant figure this out?** Yes
- **Screenshots:** Import interface, review selection screen

## What a Merchant Would Think
- If I were running a store doing $10K/month, would I pay for this? The free plan already covers everything most stores need. The $15/month Awesome plan is worth it only if you need integrations (Klaviyo, Gorgias), Q&A, or sell internationally and need translation.
- What problem does this actually solve for me? Social proof. New visitors trust reviews more than product descriptions. This app automates the collection and makes them visible where they matter.
- What's the biggest friction point in using this app? Choosing between the many widget options. The app offers 16 widgets on the paid plan, and there's no clear guidance on which combination works best for which type of store.
- What would make me uninstall this within the first week? Hard to think of a reason. The free plan is generous, setup is fast, and it works immediately. The most likely uninstall reason is switching to a competing app (Loox, Stamped) for a specific feature like UGC-focused visual galleries.

## My Honest Take
Judge.me is the best value review app on Shopify. The free plan includes features that competitors charge $20-$50/month for, including photo/video reviews, Google rich snippets, and cross-platform sync. Setup takes minutes and the email collection flow works out of the box. The AI features on the paid plan are convenient but not a selling point. Where Judge.me wins is the combination of a generous free tier and reliability: it does what it says, doesn't slow your store down, and doesn't pressure you to upgrade. For most stores under $50K/month, the free plan is enough.
