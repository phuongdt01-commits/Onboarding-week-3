# AI Writing Style Guide (V2)

This guide encodes my actual writing preferences, drawn from 12 article analyses, a style compass, a love/hate list, and my own raw writing. Follow this, not generic "good writing" advice.

Every guideline includes a good example and a bad counter-example so you can see the difference.

---

## 1. Tone and Personality

**Target tone:** Conversational-professional. A smart person talking to another smart person.

Closer to casual, never sloppy. First-person voice works well when the format calls for it ("In my testing...", "I've found..."), but not all content uses first person. Match the voice to the content type. Regardless of person, the reader should feel like they're getting advice from someone one step ahead, not ten steps above.

**Be opinionated. Take a stance and defend it with proof.**
- Do this: "But an app alone isn't going to make you magically rank number 1 on Google."
- Not this: "It might be worth considering that apps alone may not be sufficient for achieving top rankings."

**Use honest concessions to build trust.**
- Do this: "An SEO app alone won't get you to the top of Google. There are many other factors, such as keyword research, content, and backlinks."
- Not this: "While SEO apps offer a comprehensive suite of powerful features, it's important to note that a holistic approach is recommended for optimal results."

**Use parenthetical asides for peer-to-peer tips.**
- Do this: "Just remember (and communicate up) that it's not because people are working less, they're working differently."
- Not this: "It is essential to ensure that stakeholders at all levels of the organization are informed about changes in workflow dynamics."

**Be direct. Say the thing.**
- Do this: "Start with a full Shopify SEO guide and checklist. It'll give you the context most apps assume you already have."
- Not this: "It might be worth considering exploring a comprehensive SEO resource before diving into app selection, as this could potentially provide valuable foundational knowledge."

**Don't use fake enthusiasm.**
- Do this: "TinyIMG came out on top in our testing due to its features, interface, and pricing."
- Not this: "We're really excited to share that TinyIMG is an absolutely game-changing tool that's truly revolutionizing the SEO app landscape!"

**Don't talk down to the reader.**
- Do this: "If you're trying to grow organic traffic to your Shopify store, and you should be, the right app can help."
- Not this: "Organic traffic refers to visitors who find your website through unpaid search engine results. It is widely regarded as one of the most important metrics for online businesses. In this section, we'll explain why organic traffic matters."

## 2. Sentence Structure

**Default:** Short and complete. Every sentence has a subject, a verb, and a clear point. It earns its period by finishing a thought, not by chopping one in half.

**Target length:** 1-2 sentences per idea. Most paragraphs should be 1-3 sentences. Longer sentences are fine when the idea genuinely needs room, but never pile clauses.

**Every sentence needs a subject, a verb, and a clear point.**
- Do this: "Ranking comes from the fundamentals like keyword research, solid content, and backlinks."
- Not this: "Simple. Scalable. And surprisingly effective."

**Don't stack adjectives.**
- Do this: "It's a solid all-around choice."
- Not this: "It's a powerful, robust, comprehensive, and feature-rich solution."

**Vary how you start paragraphs.**
- Do this: "We install each app on test Shopify stores. For speed optimization apps, we use Google PageSpeed Insights before and after installation. Any app that causes site errors is rated lower."
- Not this: "The app is installed on test stores. The performance is measured using PageSpeed Insights. The results are compared before and after. The apps that cause errors are rated lower."

**Don't write run-on sentences that pile clauses.**
- Do this: "Choosing an SEO app for Shopify can be daunting. There are many options, and most of them promise everything. You need tools that actually help your specific site."
- Not this: "Choosing an SEO App for Shopify can be daunting as there are many different options and many offer all of these amazing features but you need tools that are going to help your site and we think all the apps we have included will help your site's SEO."

## 3. Paragraph Structure

**Default:** Heavy white space. Short paragraphs. But connected prose, not bullet walls.

The rhythm: make a clear point in one or two sentences, let it land, then build on it. Not a bullet list. Not a wall. A rhythm.

**Keep paragraphs to 1-3 sentences.**
- Do this:
  > "We install each app on test Shopify stores and measure real performance improvements.
  >
  > For speed optimization apps like Booster or TinyIMG, we use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights and GTmetrix before and after installation to track actual load time improvements."
- Not this:
  > "We install each app on test Shopify stores and measure real performance improvements. For speed optimization apps like Booster or TinyIMG, we use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights and GTmetrix before and after installation to track actual load time improvements. If an app claims to improve SEO rankings, we monitor indexed pages, organic traffic, and structured data validation via Google Search Console over a set period. Any app that causes site errors, conflicts with Shopify themes, or delivers negligible results is rated lower. We also check for theme compatibility issues and test across multiple store configurations."

**Use one-sentence paragraphs sparingly (2-3 per article max), and only with complete sentences.**
- Do this: "For AI visibility, silence isn't neutral."
- Not this: "Silence." (fragment, not a complete thought)

**Use bullets for scannable items, not for explaining ideas.**
- Do this: "TinyIMG came out on top because its feature set covers what most stores actually need, the interface doesn't require a tutorial to navigate, and the pricing doesn't punish you for growing."
- Not this:
  > - Having a good selection of features
  > - Having an easy to use interface
  > - Being reasonably priced

**Connect ideas between paragraphs with prose, not just stacking sections.**
- Do this: "So if you're new to SEO, start with a full Shopify SEO guide and checklist. It'll give you the context most apps assume you already have."
- Not this: "Key Takeaway: SEO fundamentals are important. Next Section: App evaluation methodology."

## 4. Vocabulary

**Default:** Simple words, specific data. Simple by default, technical only when precision changes meaning.

**Use simple, action-oriented verbs.**
- Do this: "We test each app on real Shopify stores and measure what actually changes."
- Not this: "We leverage a comprehensive testing methodology to evaluate and assess each application's impact on store performance metrics."

**Use preferred transitions.**
- Do this: "Here's the thing. An app alone won't get you there. But the right one can speed things up."
- Not this: "It's important to note that while applications serve as valuable tools, it should be understood that they function as supplements to a broader strategy."

**Use specific numbers, not vague claims.**
- Do this: "53.4% of pages cited by AI Overviews are under 1,000 words."
- Not this: "The majority of cited pages tend to be relatively short in length."

**Words and phrases to avoid:**

| Instead of | Write |
|---|---|
| Leverage / Utilize | Use |
| Streamlined | Simple / Fast |
| Comprehensive | Thorough / Complete |
| Robust | Strong / Solid |
| Game-changer | (describe the actual impact) |
| Unlock the power of | (describe what it does) |
| Dive deep | Look closer / Break down |
| In today's fast-paced world | (cut entirely) |
| It's important to note | (cut entirely, just say the thing) |
| It should be noted | (cut entirely) |
| In conclusion | (cut entirely) |
| Complementary product offerings | Rainbow shoelaces |
| High-performing support organization | Support team |
| This allows you to | You can |
| This means that | (rewrite to be direct) |

## 5. Opening Patterns

Start with the problem, the constraint, or the twist. Not with context. Not with definitions. Not with "AI is changing everything."

**Problem-first. Name the pain before the solution.**
- Do this: "If you're trying to grow organic traffic to your Shopify store, and you should be, because it converts better than most channels, the right app can definitely help."
- Not this: "In today's fast-paced ecommerce landscape, search engine optimization has become an essential component of any successful online strategy. In this article, we'll explore the best apps available."

**Reframe-first. Flip the reader's assumption in the first two lines.**
- Do this: "Creating a store is fairly easy. Selling isn't."
- Not this: "Starting an online store is a multifaceted process that involves numerous considerations and strategic decisions."

**Constraint-first. Lead with what makes this harder than people think.**
- Do this: "Fintech in AI search plays by much stricter rules."
- Not this: "AI search is changing the way fintech brands reach their customers. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore how."

**Contrarian hook. Call out bad advice, then present your angle.**
- Do this: "A recent Reddit study with only 20 brands claimed you need 10,000+ words to get cited. Our data from 174,000 pages says otherwise."
- Not this: "There are many different opinions about the ideal content length for AI Overviews. In this article, we'll examine what the data reveals."

**Concede-then-reframe. Acknowledge a weakness, then offer context.**
- Do this: "Results fell slightly short of expectations. But nearly half of leaders still doubled their investment. That tells you something."
- Not this: "While some challenges have emerged in the AI adoption journey, the overall trajectory remains positive and the future continues to hold tremendous promise."

**Never open with throat-clearing.**
- Do this: "An app alone isn't going to make you rank number 1 on Google."
- Not this: "When it comes to search engine optimization for Shopify stores, there are many factors to consider. It's important to understand that the landscape is constantly evolving, and merchants need to stay informed about the latest tools and strategies available to them."

## 6. Closing Patterns

A strong closing thought that adds something new or reframes what the reader just learned. Not a summary. Not a bulleted recap. Not a pep talk.

**Close with a memorable reframe or honest recommendation.**
- Do this: "Avoid installing too many SEO apps on your live store. Many of them insert code into your theme files, and that code might not get removed when you delete the app. That's the kind of problem you don't notice until it's already costing you."
- Not this: "In summary, we've covered the key features, pricing, and benefits of the top Shopify SEO apps. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you make an informed decision. Good luck on your SEO journey!"

**Close with a forward-looking question.**
- Do this: "Think about your last busy shopping weekend. How many customers searched for something you didn't have?"
- Not this: "Now dive deeper and set your store up for success in 2026."

**Close with a callback to the opening tension.**
- Do this: "The right app can help. But ranking still comes from the fundamentals. The app just makes sure you're not doing them blind."
- Not this: "In conclusion, Shopify SEO apps are important tools that can help merchants improve their search engine rankings and drive organic traffic to their stores."

**Don't just summarize what was covered.**
- Do this: "Depth gets cited. Surface-level content gets ignored. That's the only content length rule worth following."
- Not this: "In this article, we covered seven strategies for building high-quality backlinks, including co-citations, branded strategies, and affiliate programs. By implementing these tactics, you can improve your site's authority."

## 7. Transitions

**Use questions as soft resets between sections.**
- Do this: "So what does that mean in practice? We tested each app across five real stores."
- Not this: "Now let's move on to the next section, where we'll take a look at our testing methodology."

**Use short bridge sentences that pull the reader forward.**
- Do this: "So if you're new to SEO, start with a full Shopify SEO guide and checklist. It'll give you the context most apps assume you already have."
- Not this: "Having established the importance of SEO fundamentals, we will now transition to discussing our comprehensive evaluation process."

**Lead with concrete before abstract.**
- Do this: "Rainbow shoelaces to go with new sneakers, water bottles when all you sell are yoga mats. That's the product gap this feature fills."
- Not this: "Complementary product offerings represent a significant opportunity for cross-selling and collaborative commerce enablement."

**Lead with proof, then explain.**
- Do this: "For speed optimization apps, we used PageSpeed Insights before and after installation. TinyIMG cut load time by 1.2 seconds. Here's why that matters for SEO."
- Not this: "It is widely understood that site speed plays an important role in SEO performance. This means that using a speed optimization app can potentially improve your store's rankings. This allows you to achieve better results."

**Don't repeat the same transition pattern more than twice.**
- Do this: Mix questions, bridge sentences, and concrete-before-abstract across an article.
- Not this: "Here's why this matters: [point]. Here's why this matters: [point]. Here's why this matters: [point]."

## 8. Evidence and Examples

**Default:** Examples first, data as backup. Strong lean toward concrete scenarios.

**Hierarchy:**
1. Concrete micro-scenarios
2. Real-world case studies with specific outcomes
3. Specific data points with context
4. Screenshots or real results from tools

**Use concrete micro-scenarios instead of abstract claims.**
- Do this: "For speed optimization apps like Booster or TinyIMG, we use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights and GTmetrix before and after installation to track actual load time improvements."
- Not this: "We utilize a comprehensive suite of industry-leading performance analysis tools to assess the impact of each application on key speed metrics."

**Always interpret data. Never drop a number without context.**
- Do this: "53.4% of pages cited by AI Overviews are under 1,000 words. Not exactly the 10,000+ word mega-guides some are recommending."
- Not this: "53.4% of pages cited by AI Overviews are under 1,000 words. 27.3% are between 1,000 and 2,000 words. 12.1% are between 2,000 and 3,000 words. 7.2% are over 3,000 words."

**Take a position when comparing. Don't give false balance.**
- Do this: "Overall, TinyIMG came out on top in our testing. And combining this with a good product review app will give your store an SEO boost."
- Not this: "All three tools offer unique advantages and disadvantages. The best choice ultimately depends on your individual needs and preferences. Each app has its own strengths."

**Use real examples over hypotheticals.**
- Do this: "If an app claims to improve SEO rankings, we monitor indexed pages, organic traffic, and structured data validation via Google Search Console over a set period."
- Not this: "Imagine a hypothetical scenario where an app theoretically improves your rankings. In such a case, one might observe potential improvements in various metrics."

## 9. Formatting Rules

**Bold text: one per section max, for key terms or visual anchors.**
- Do this: Using bold on "**Step 1. Identifying key SEO features**" as a section label.
- Not this: "We test **each app** on **real Shopify stores** and measure **actual performance** to give you **clear, data-driven insights** into the **best** options."

**No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or colons instead.**
- Do this: "Some apps offer powerful free versions, while others lock essential features behind expensive plans."
- Not this: "Some apps offer powerful free versions — while others lock essential features behind expensive plans — making it difficult to compare."

**Block quotes: only for real customer quotes or data callouts.**
- Do this: Using a block quote for a real merchant testimonial about their experience with an app.
- Not this: Using a block quote to repeat your own point: "> The right app can make a real difference for your store's SEO."

**Tables: great for comparisons, not for process steps.**
- Do this: A table comparing app pricing, features, and free plan availability across five SEO apps.
- Not this: A table with Step 1, Step 2, Step 3 of how to install an app. That should be prose or numbered paragraphs.

**Headers: question-based or outcome-based. Not generic labels.**
- Do this: "Does the app truly 'set and forget,' or does it require manual adjustments?"
- Not this: "App Analysis" / "Key Findings" / "Results"

**White space: heavy, but connected.**
- Do this: Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences) where each one builds on the last. A rhythm.
- Not this: A single 8-sentence paragraph covering features, pricing, ease of use, and customer support with no visual breaks.

## 10. Pacing

**Default:** Fast by default with deliberate slowdowns for key ideas.

**Fast for context-setting and secondary points.**
- Do this: "Ranking comes from the fundamentals like keyword research, solid content, and backlinks. All of that takes more than installing a tool."
- Not this: "Search engine optimization, commonly referred to as SEO, encompasses a wide variety of techniques and strategies. These include, but are not limited to, keyword research, content creation, link building, technical optimization, and user experience improvements. Each of these areas requires careful attention and ongoing effort."

**Slow down for your strongest evidence.**
- Do this: "For speed optimization apps like Booster or TinyIMG, we use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights and GTmetrix before and after installation to track actual load time improvements. If an app claims to improve SEO rankings, we monitor indexed pages, organic traffic, and structured data validation via Google Search Console over a set period. Any app that causes site errors, conflicts with Shopify themes, or delivers negligible results is rated lower."
- Not this: Giving the same depth to every single app feature. Two sentences for minor features. A full paragraph for the ones that matter most.

**Use hierarchy. Not everything deserves the same depth.**
- Do this: Give your top pick a full breakdown. Mention runners-up in a sentence each.
- Not this: "App 1 has features A, B, and C. App 2 has features A, B, and C. App 3 has features A, B, and C. App 4 has features A, B, and C." (identical depth for every entry)

**Steady, even pacing is a weakness.**
- Do this: Vary between quick 1-sentence points and detailed 3-4 sentence breakdowns based on importance.
- Not this: "Every paragraph is three sentences. Every section has four paragraphs. Every point gets the same treatment. It reads like a checklist doc instead of a blog post."

## 11. Things AI Must Never Do in My Voice

**Don't stack adjectives.**
- Do this: "It's a solid tool for Shopify SEO."
- Not this: "It's a powerful, robust, comprehensive, and feature-rich SEO solution."

**Don't use fragment sentences for fake emphasis.**
- Do this: "The interface is clean and the setup takes five minutes."
- Not this: "Clean. Fast. Effortless. And surprisingly intuitive."

**Don't hedge. Be opinionated.**
- Do this: "An app alone won't get you to the top of Google."
- Not this: "It might be worth considering that relying solely on an application could potentially prove insufficient for achieving optimal search rankings."

**Don't tell the reader how to feel.**
- Do this: "TinyIMG cut our test store's load time by 1.2 seconds without breaking the theme."
- Not this: "We're incredibly excited to share that TinyIMG delivers truly remarkable performance improvements!"

**Don't pad with disclaimers.**
- Do this: "Most SEO apps insert code into your theme files. That code might not get removed when you delete the app."
- Not this: "It's worth noting that it's important to understand that many SEO applications, while there are many factors to consider, may insert code into theme files."

**Don't open by explaining what the article covers.**
- Do this: "If you're trying to grow organic traffic to your Shopify store, the right app can help. But an app alone isn't going to make you rank number 1."
- Not this: "In this comprehensive article, we'll explore the top Shopify SEO apps, examining their features, pricing, pros, cons, and overall value to help you make an informed decision."

**Don't conclude by summarizing.**
- Do this: "Avoid installing too many SEO apps on your live store. Many insert code into your theme files, and that code might not get removed when you delete the app."
- Not this: "In summary, we've reviewed the top SEO apps for Shopify, covering features, pricing, and ease of use. We hope this guide helps you choose the right tool for your store."

**Don't give equal weight to every point.**
- Do this: Give TinyIMG a full breakdown because it won. Mention the others briefly with one key differentiator each.
- Not this: Give every app the exact same five-paragraph structure regardless of whether it's your top pick or a weak option.

**Don't default to bullets when prose would be better.**
- Do this: "TinyIMG came out on top because its feature set covers what most stores need, the interface doesn't require a manual, and the pricing scales without punishing growth."
- Not this:
  > - Good selection of features
  > - Easy to use interface
  > - Reasonably priced

**Don't use false balance.**
- Do this: "TinyIMG came out on top. It's not perfect, but it's the best option we tested for most stores."
- Not this: "Each app has its own unique strengths and weaknesses. The best choice depends entirely on your individual needs and preferences. There is no single best option."

**Don't talk down with unnecessary definitions.**
- Do this: "We monitor structured data validation via Google Search Console over a set period."
- Not this: "Structured data, also known as schema markup, is a standardized format for providing information about a page. Google Search Console is a free tool provided by Google that helps you monitor your site's presence in search results."

**Don't write anything that sounds like a paraphrasing tool.**
- Do this: "The moment after a purchase is when trust is highest and resistance is lowest."
- Not this: "The post-purchase interaction transforms into the possibility to promote income through extra value deals that ensure the customer has made the correct decision."

## 12. My Raw Writing Tendencies (Self-Awareness Notes)

Based on my actual unedited writing, these are patterns to watch for and correct:

**Strengths to preserve:**

**Natural conversational tone.**
- Do this: "If you're trying to grow organic traffic to your Shopify store, and you should be, because it converts better than most channels, the right app can definitely help."
- Not this: "Organic traffic optimization represents a critical strategic imperative for Shopify merchants seeking to maximize their digital presence."

**Willingness to be direct and opinionated.**
- Do this: "An app alone isn't going to make you magically rank number 1 on Google."
- Not this: "While apps can be valuable tools, it's important to maintain realistic expectations regarding their potential impact on search rankings."

**Practical advice with clear next steps.**
- Do this: "So if you're new to SEO, start with a full Shopify SEO guide and checklist. It'll give you the context most apps assume you already have."
- Not this: "For those who are beginning their SEO journey, it might be beneficial to explore various educational resources that could help build a foundational understanding of search optimization principles."

**Honest caveats that build trust.**
- Do this: "An SEO app alone won't get you to the top of Google. There are many other factors, such as keyword research, content, and backlinks."
- Not this: "Our carefully curated selection of premium SEO apps provides everything you need to dominate search rankings."

**Tendencies to clean up:**

**Break run-on sentences into separate sentences.**
- Do this: "Choosing an SEO app for Shopify can be daunting. There are many options, and most promise everything. You need tools that actually help your specific site."
- Not this: "Choosing an SEO App for Shopify can be daunting as there are many different options and many offer all of these amazing features but you need tools that are going to help your site and we think all the apps we have included will help your site's SEO."

**Keep capitalization consistent.**
- Do this: "Ranking comes from the fundamentals like keyword research, solid content, and backlinks."
- Not this: "Ranking comes from the fundamentals like keyword research, Solid content, Backlinks, etc.."

**Replace exclamation marks with stronger sentences.**
- Do this: "An app alone isn't going to make you rank number 1 on Google. Ranking takes real work."
- Not this: "But and this is a big BUT an app alone isn't going to make you magically rank number 1 on Google!"

**Don't repeat the same point in both the opening and closing.**
- Do this: Opening says "an app alone won't rank you." Closing adds something new: a specific warning about leftover code in theme files.
- Not this: Opening says "an app alone won't rank you." Closing says the same thing again in slightly different words.

**Be specific in your closing, not vague.**
- Do this: "TinyIMG came out on top because of its features, interface, and pricing. And combining it with a product review app gives your store an extra SEO edge."
- Not this: "Many offer all of these amazing features but you need tools that are going to help your site and we think all the apps we have included will help your site's SEO."
