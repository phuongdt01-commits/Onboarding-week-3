# Why Most People Fail at Building Habits (And How to Fix It)

You've done this before. January 1st, a Monday morning, or the day after a particularly bad week. You pick a habit, you commit hard, and for about two weeks it actually sticks. Then one missed day becomes two, and the whole thing quietly dies.

The problem isn't you. It's the strategy. Most habit advice boils down to "be more disciplined," which is like telling someone who can't swim to just try harder in deep water. Willpower is a limited resource, and every study on the subject confirms it burns out fast.

There's a better approach, and it doesn't require more motivation. It requires less. The idea is simple: instead of forcing yourself to make good choices, redesign your environment so the good choice is the easy one.
