# Why Most People Fail at Building Habits (And How to Fix It)

## Introduction
- Hook the reader — the "fresh start" cycle most people know too well
- State the problem — willpower-based approaches have a near-100% failure rate
- Preview the solution — environment design beats motivation every time

## What Is Environment Design?
- Explain the concept — shaping your surroundings so the desired behavior becomes the path of least resistance
- Why it matters — decisions are made before you "decide"; context drives behavior far more than intention

## How to Redesign Your Environment for Better Habits
- Step-by-step: the friction framework — add friction to bad habits, remove it from good ones
- Examples — putting your phone in another room vs. relying on screen-time apps; placing a book on your pillow instead of setting a "read more" goal

## Why Willpower Alone Keeps Failing
- Evidence — ego depletion research and real-world dropout rates from resolution studies
- Common mistakes — stacking too many habits at once, relying on reminders instead of restructuring, ignoring the role of social environment

## Conclusion
- Key takeaway — stop trying to be more disciplined; start making the right choice the easy choice
- What to do next — pick one habit, identify its biggest friction point, and change that single thing today
