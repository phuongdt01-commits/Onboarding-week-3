## Scoring Guide

Rate each criterion from 1-10. Use the benchmarks below to calibrate.

---

### 1. Voice Consistency

_Does the writing match the Style Guide tone? Conversational-professional, peer-to-peer, opinionated with evidence._

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Reads like corporate copy or AI output. Formal, hedging, no personality. |
| 5 | Tone is mostly right but drifts into generic phrasing in spots. Some hedging or disclaimer padding slips through. |
| 7 | Consistent conversational-professional tone throughout. Direct, opinionated, with honest concessions where needed. |
| 9 | Voice is sharp and unmistakable. Every sentence sounds like a smart person giving peer-to-peer advice. No false balance, no talking down. |

**Score:** __/10

### 2. Hook Strength

_Does the intro create tension or stakes within the first two sentences? Does it make the reader feel something, not just describe a topic?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Opens with throat-clearing, a definition, or "In today's..." No reason to keep reading. |
| 5 | Names a problem but without stakes. The reader understands the topic but doesn't feel pulled in. |
| 7 | Strong problem-first or reframe-first hook. The reader knows why this matters to them within two sentences. |
| 9 | The opener flips an assumption or creates real tension. The reader can't skip to the next tab. |

**Score:** __/10
**Notes:**

---

### 3. Structure and Flow

_Does the piece progress logically? Do transitions connect sections without repeating the same pattern? Does pacing vary between sections?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Sections feel random or disconnected. Same transition pattern repeated throughout. Every section is the same length and weight. |
| 5 | Logical order but transitions are mechanical ("Let's look at..." / "Now let's move on to..."). Pacing is mostly uniform. |
| 7 | Clear progression with varied transitions. Key ideas get full breakdowns while secondary points stay brief. Sections connect through bridge sentences, questions, or direct pivots. |
| 9 | Structure feels inevitable. Each section builds on the last. Pacing has deliberate rhythm: fast for context, slow for the strongest evidence. Transitions are invisible. |

**Score:** __/10
**Notes:**

---

### 4. Accuracy

_Are claims backed by specific, named sources? Are numbers precise? Is any data presented without interpretation?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Vague authority throughout ("studies show," "experts agree"). Claims with no supporting evidence. |
| 5 | Some sources named, but a few vague claims remain. Data is present but occasionally missing interpretation. |
| 7 | All major claims cite specific sources. Every stat has a plain-language interpretation. No vague authority. |
| 9 | Every claim is sourced and verifiable. Data points include context that explains why they matter. Evidence hierarchy is clear: concrete examples first, data as backup. |

**Score:** __/10
**Notes:**

### 5. Readability

_Are sentences short and complete? Are paragraphs 1-3 sentences with heavy white space? Is connected prose used instead of bullet walls?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Wall-of-text paragraphs or bullet walls with no connective tissue. Run-on sentences stacking clauses with "and" and "but." Fragment sentences used for fake drama. |
| 5 | Paragraphs are a reasonable length but some run long. A few run-on sentences. Occasional bullet lists where prose would work better. |
| 7 | Short paragraphs with white space. Sentences are complete and carry one idea each. Bullets reserved for genuinely scannable items. |
| 9 | Every paragraph earns its length. Sentence rhythm varies naturally. White space is generous. Ideas live in connected prose with clear breathing room between them. |

**Score:** __/10
**Notes:**

### 6. Formatting

_Are headings question-based or outcome-based? Is bold used sparingly? Are tables and block quotes used only where they belong?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Generic headings ("Overview," "Results"). Bold on every other sentence. Tables or block quotes used decoratively. Em dashes present. |
| 5 | Headings are mostly descriptive but a few are generic. Bold is overused in some sections. Formatting is functional but not strategic. |
| 7 | Headings are specific and useful for scanning. Bold highlights key terms without cluttering. Tables used only for comparisons. No em dashes. |
| 9 | Headings alone tell the story of the article. Formatting choices are invisible because they serve the reader perfectly. Every visual element earns its place. |

**Score:** __/10
**Notes:**

### 7. Banned Phrase Check

_Does the piece contain any vocabulary or patterns from the Style Guide's banned lists?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Multiple banned words or phrases ("leverage," "comprehensive," "game-changer," "it's important to note"). Corporate filler throughout. |
| 5 | One or two banned phrases slip through. Some filler setup lines ("There's a better way," "The idea is simple") remain. |
| 7 | No banned vocabulary. No corporate filler. Occasional phrasing that borders on generic but doesn't cross the line. |
| 9 | Every word choice is precise and intentional. Zero banned phrases, zero filler, zero hedging. The piece would pass a line-by-line audit against the full banned list. |

### 8. CTA and Conclusion

_Does the closing land on something specific and sharp? Does the CTA give the reader one clear next action?_

| Score | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| 3 | Ends with a summary ("In this article, we covered...") or generic advice ("Start small and be consistent"). CTA is missing or boilerplate. |
| 5 | Closing has a point but drifts into vague territory. CTA exists but feels disconnected from the piece. |
| 7 | Closing adds something new or reframes the piece. CTA is specific and follows naturally from the content. No recap, no bullet summary. |
| 9 | Final paragraph is the most memorable part of the article. The reader leaves with a specific thought or action they didn't have before. CTA feels like the obvious next step. |
