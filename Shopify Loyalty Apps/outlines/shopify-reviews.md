## H2: Why You're Not Getting Reviews (It's Not Your Product) (~250 words)

### H3: The Friction Problem
- Default Shopify review flow requires too many clicks
- Customers have to navigate back to the product page, scroll to the review section, and sometimes create an account
- Every extra step loses people

### H3: The Timing Problem
- Asking before delivery = ignored. Asking 30 days later = forgotten.
- The sweet spot is 7–14 days after delivery confirmation

### H3: The Negativity Bias
- Unhappy customers are self-motivated to leave reviews; happy ones need a nudge
- This skews your review profile if you only collect organic reviews
- The fix isn't suppressing bad reviews — it's actively collecting from the satisfied majority

## H2: 5 Free Strategies to Start Collecting Reviews Today (~400 words)

### H3: 1. The Post-Purchase Email (Your Highest-ROI Move)
- Send a short, personal email 7–14 days after delivery
- Include a direct link to the specific product's review form — not your homepage
- Keep it to 3–4 sentences. Subject line matters: "How's your [Product Name] working out?" outperforms "Leave us a review"
- Tip: Shopify's order status automations can trigger these without an app

> [EXAMPLE SPOT: Sample review request email — short, personal, with a clear CTA button]

### H3: 2. Package Inserts with QR Codes
- Include a small card in every shipment with a QR code linking directly to the review page
- Add a human touch: "We're a small team and your feedback genuinely helps us improve"
- Low cost, high visibility — the customer sees it at the moment of unboxing excitement

> [SCREENSHOT SPOT: Example package insert card design with QR code]

### H3: 3. Social Media Follow-Ups
- When customers tag you on Instagram or TikTok, reply thanking them and ask if they'd share that feedback as a store review
- Turns existing UGC into on-site social proof

### H3: 4. Personal Outreach for High-Value Orders
- For orders above a certain threshold, send a personal (not templated) thank-you message
- People who spend more are often more engaged and willing to share their experience

### H3: 5. Incentivize with a Discount Code
- Offer 10–15% off the next order in exchange for an honest review
- FTC compliance note: incentivized reviews must be disclosed — be transparent about it
- Frame it as a thank-you, not a transaction: "As a thank-you for sharing your experience, here's 10% off your next order"

## H2: When to Upgrade — Review Apps That Automate the Work (~450 words)

- Transition: Manual strategies work, but they don't scale. If you're processing 50+ orders/month, an app pays for itself in time saved.

### H3: What Review Apps Actually Do
- Automated, timed review request emails and SMS after fulfillment
- In-email review forms — customers leave a rating without leaving their inbox
- Photo and video review collection with optional incentives
- Display widgets: star ratings on collection pages, review carousels, dedicated review pages
- Syndication to Google Shopping, Facebook, and Instagram

> [SCREENSHOT SPOT: Side-by-side of a basic Shopify review section vs. a rich review widget with photos, filters, and star breakdowns]

### H3: 5 Top Review Apps for Shopify

**Judge.me** — Best free option
- Free plan available; paid plan ~$15/month. 5.0 stars on Shopify App Store.
- In-email forms, photo/video reviews, Google Shopping integration, 38+ languages
- Fastest-loading option — no impact on site speed

**Loox** — Best for visual/photo-heavy stores
- From $12.99/month
- Instagram-style photo review displays, referral features, one-click email reviews

**Stamped.io** — Best for stores that also want Q&A
- From ~$23/month (no free plan)
- Reviews + Q&A + NPS surveys, loyalty program integration, Google Seller Ratings

**Yotpo** — Best all-in-one platform
- Free for up to 50 orders/month; paid from ~$15/month
- Reviews, loyalty, referrals, SMS — one platform for everything. AI-optimized request timing.

**Okendo** — Best for detailed customer profiles
- From ~$19/month
- Collects customer attributes (skin type, use case, etc.) for richer, more useful reviews. Strong Klaviyo integration.

> [SCREENSHOT SPOT: Comparison table of the 5 apps — columns: App, Starting Price, Free Plan, Key Strength]

### H3: How to Choose
- Under 50 orders/month → start with Judge.me (free) or Yotpo (free tier)
- Visual products (fashion, beauty, home) → Loox or Okendo
- Need loyalty + reviews in one tool → Yotpo or Stamped.io
- Heavy Klaviyo user → Okendo

## H2: Make the Most of the Reviews You Collect (~200 words)

### H3: Display Them Everywhere
- Star ratings on collection pages (not just product pages)
- Review carousel on homepage
- Pull best quotes into ad copy and email campaigns
- Syndicate to Google Shopping for star ratings in search results

### H3: Respond to Every Negative Review
- Don't delete — respond publicly and constructively
- [VERIFY] 45% of consumers are more likely to visit a business that responds to negative reviews (ReviewTrackers)
- A thoughtful response to a 2-star review builds more trust than ten 5-star reviews with no reply

## H2: Your Next Steps — Start This Week (~150 words)

1. **Today**: Set up a post-purchase email in Shopify that goes out 7 days after fulfillment with a direct review link
2. **This week**: Design a simple package insert card with a QR code (Canva has free templates)
3. **This month**: Install Judge.me or Yotpo's free plan and let automation take over
4. **Ongoing**: Respond to every review — positive and negative — within 48 hours
5. **Once you hit 50+ reviews**: Add star ratings to collection pages and start syndicating to Google Shopping

- Closing: Reviews compound. The first 10 are the hardest. Once social proof is visible, more customers leave reviews organically because they see others doing it. Start small, be consistent, and let the flywheel build.
