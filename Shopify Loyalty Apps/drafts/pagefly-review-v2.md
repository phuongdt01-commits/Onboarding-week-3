**PageFly** is a Shopify page builder designed for merchants who want more control over store design without relying on custom code. With its drag-and-drop editor, you can create landing pages, product pages, homepages, blog pages, and other custom layouts that go beyond what Shopify's default editor typically allows. One thing to know upfront: PageFly is Shopify-only. If you're running your store on WooCommerce or BigCommerce, this isn't for you.

## PageFly Features That Actually Matter for Shopify Stores

Not every feature on the list matters equally. Below, we focus on the ones that had the most tangible impact during our testing — and explain what each one actually saves you or makes possible.

### Drag-and-Drop Editor With 350+ Elements

PageFly gives you access to more than 350 elements, from basic content blocks like text, images, and buttons to conversion-focused modules such as countdown timers, product recommendations, and QR codes. In practical terms, that means you can build most page layouts — promotional banners, product showcases, trust sections — without installing separate apps or writing custom code, which reduces both your app costs and your dependency on developers.

Where this matters most is layout flexibility. Shopify's native editor limits you to predefined section structures, so building a page that doesn't match your theme's default layout often requires either custom Liquid code or a workaround. PageFly removes that constraint by letting you arrange, group, and space elements freely based on the page you actually want to build. For stores running frequent campaigns or launching new products, that flexibility translates directly into faster page turnaround — you spend less time fighting layout restrictions and more time getting pages live.

### Pre-Built Templates (100+)

PageFly offers more than 100 templates organized by page type and industry, which helps you skip the blank-canvas phase and start with a layout that already follows conversion best practices. The landing page and product page templates, in particular, come with built-in sections for reviews, trust signals, and calls to action — elements that typically take extra time to set up manually. By contrast, several blog and about page templates felt more like functional starting points than fully developed designs.

The bigger time-saver is that templates auto-adapt to your store's products. When you pick a product page template, PageFly pulls in your product images, prices, and descriptions automatically. In our test, this cut the initial setup of a product page from roughly 40 minutes down to under 15 — a meaningful difference if you're building pages for multiple products or running time-sensitive campaigns.

### Built-In A/B Testing

One of PageFly's most valuable features is built-in A/B testing, available starting at the $18/month plan. Most competing page builders either exclude split testing from lower-tier plans or charge significantly more for it — Shogun, for example, locks it behind a $149/month tier. Having A/B testing built into the page builder itself means you can test different headlines, layouts, CTAs, or entirely different page versions without paying for a separate optimization tool, which saves both cost and the complexity of connecting third-party testing software.

The tool uses Bayesian statistics to determine winning variants, and while it won't replace a dedicated CRO platform like VWO or Convert for advanced funnel testing, it covers the most common use case well: figuring out which version of a landing page or product page converts better. For stores that aren't ready to invest in a full CRO stack, this feature alone can justify the monthly cost by helping you identify what works before scaling ad spend.

### Mobile Responsiveness

With more than 70% of Shopify traffic now coming from mobile devices, how a page looks on smaller screens directly affects your conversion rate. PageFly addresses this with device-specific editing, which lets you customize the desktop, tablet, and mobile versions of a page separately rather than relying on automatic responsive scaling that often breaks spacing or hides important content.

In our testing, pages looked clean on mobile without extra work. Beyond that, the ability to hide desktop-only elements, adjust font sizes, and rearrange sections per device meant we could optimize the mobile experience without compromising the desktop layout — something that's difficult to do in Shopify's native editor without custom CSS. For advanced users, PageFly also supports custom HTML, CSS, and JavaScript directly in the editor, which adds another layer of control when templates and drag-and-drop aren't enough.

### 130+ App Integrations

If you're already running Shopify apps like Klaviyo for email, Judge.me or Loox for reviews, or Ali Reviews for social proof, PageFly integrates with all of them — over 130 apps in total. Because these integrations work through app blocks and embedded widgets, you can embed review carousels, email capture forms, or upsell modules directly into your PageFly pages without writing any code or manually configuring each connection.

The practical benefit is that your custom-built pages don't exist in isolation from the rest of your store's marketing stack. A landing page can display live product reviews from Judge.me, trigger a Klaviyo popup, and show a loyalty widget — all within the same PageFly layout. That eliminates the friction of managing these elements separately and ensures that your highest-converting pages have access to the same tools as the rest of your store.

## The Honest Pros and Cons of PageFly

Features only tell part of the story. Here's what the day-to-day experience actually looks like — both the strengths and the trade-offs.

### What PageFly Does Well

- **Design flexibility that Shopify's editor can't match.** Free-form placement lets you position elements exactly where you want them, rather than working within a fixed grid. For stores that need custom layouts for campaigns or product launches, this level of control is the primary reason to choose PageFly over alternatives.
- **A genuinely useful free plan.** You get one published page with full feature access and 24/7 live chat support — enough to build a landing page, test the editor, and measure real results before spending anything.
- **Fast, responsive customer support.** Every plan includes 24/7 live chat, including the free tier. Across hundreds of App Store reviews, users consistently name specific agents — Logan, Marcel, Shami, Kate, Ayla — for going beyond basic troubleshooting, and that kind of recurring, specific praise is difficult to manufacture.
- **Works with every Shopify theme.** PageFly integrates through app blocks on OS 2.0, so it layers on top of whatever theme you're already running without creating lock-in or requiring a theme switch.
- **Built-in A/B testing at the $18/month tier.** Most competitors charge $100 or more for split testing, so having it available at PageFly's base paid plan makes it significantly more accessible for smaller stores that want to optimize without paying for a separate CRO tool.

### Where PageFly Falls Short

- **The learning curve is steeper than expected.** With 350+ elements and free-form placement, PageFly gives you a lot of creative power — but that same openness can overwhelm beginners who are used to more structured editors. Expect your first hour to feel more like exploring than producing.
- **Leftover code when you uninstall.** If you ever remove PageFly, it leaves behind HTML and CSS in your theme files. Cleaning it up requires editing theme code directly or hiring a developer — something most reviews don't mention but is worth knowing before you commit.
- **Page speed takes a measurable hit.** Because PageFly injects additional code into every page you build with it, performance decreases as you add more elements. Lazy loading helps offset this, but our test page scored 78 on PageSpeed Insights — a noticeable gap compared with native theme pages that typically land in the 90s.
- **Shopify only, with no portability.** There is no WooCommerce, BigCommerce, or standalone version. If you ever migrate off Shopify, your PageFly pages and layouts don't come with you.
- **Premium support is locked behind the top plan.** While every plan gets 24/7 live chat — which is solid — dedicated support specialists and priority handling are only available on the Unlimited plan at $99/month.

> **Bottom line:** PageFly offers the most design freedom of any Shopify page builder we tested, and the free plan is generous enough to evaluate before committing. The trade-offs — a steeper learning curve, measurable speed impact, and leftover code on uninstall — are real but manageable for most stores that need more than what Shopify's native editor provides.

Not every store needs a page builder — and installing one when your theme already handles your layouts well only adds unnecessary complexity. Here's how to tell whether PageFly belongs in your stack.

### Best for:

- **Store owners who've outgrown the native theme editor.** If you regularly hit layout limitations when building landing pages or product pages, PageFly fills that gap directly by giving you the design control Shopify's editor lacks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is PageFly worth it in 2026?**

For most Shopify store owners, yes. If you need custom landing pages or product pages that go beyond what the native editor can do, PageFly gives you that control starting at $18/month — far cheaper than hiring a developer. The free plan also lets you test it with full features before spending anything, so you can evaluate the impact on your own store before committing.

**Does PageFly slow down your Shopify store?**

It can, depending on how you build. Our test page with a moderate number of elements scored 78 on Google PageSpeed Insights — solid, but lower than native theme pages that typically score in the 90s. The more elements you stack — particularly animations, videos, and heavy image sections — the more load time increases. PageFly includes lazy loading to help offset this, but you'll still need to keep an eye on element count for high-traffic pages.

**Can I use PageFly for free?**

Yes. The free plan gives you one published "slot" — either one page or one section — with access to every feature, every template, and 24/7 live chat. There's no time limit, so you can use it indefinitely. You'll only need to move to the $18/month plan when you want more than one published page.

**What happens if I uninstall PageFly?**

Your published pages stay live, but they may lose custom styling since PageFly's CSS gets removed during uninstallation. The larger concern is that PageFly leaves behind HTML and CSS in your theme files, which requires editing theme code directly or hiring a developer to clean up. This is worth knowing before you commit, especially if you think you might switch to a different page builder later.

**Is PageFly better than Shopify's built-in theme editor?**

They solve different problems. The native editor is faster and lighter for basic layout tasks like rearranging sections, changing colors, and swapping images. PageFly, on the other hand, is designed for situations where you need more control: free-form element placement, 350+ components, A/B testing, and layouts that don't exist in your theme's default options. If you've found yourself thinking "I wish I could just move this *here*" while working in Shopify's editor — that's exactly the gap PageFly is built to fill.

**Does PageFly work with all Shopify themes?**

Yes. PageFly is compatible with every Shopify theme, including Online Store 2.0 themes. It integrates through app blocks, so it layers on top of your existing theme without replacing it or requiring a theme switch.

**PageFly vs EComposer: which is better?**

Both are strong options, but they serve slightly different priorities. EComposer is $2/month cheaper and bundles 30+ CRO extensions — countdown bars, upsell popups, cart goals — that PageFly handles through third-party apps. PageFly, in turn, offers more design elements (350+ vs 300+), a longer track record, and more granular free-form layout control. Pick EComposer if built-in conversion tools matter most; pick PageFly if you want maximum design flexibility.

**Does PageFly support A/B testing?**

Yes — on paid plans starting at $18/month. PageFly uses Bayesian statistics to determine winning variants, which is reliable enough for testing headlines, layouts, and CTA placements. For more advanced multi-step funnel testing, however, you'd want a dedicated CRO tool like VWO or Convert.
