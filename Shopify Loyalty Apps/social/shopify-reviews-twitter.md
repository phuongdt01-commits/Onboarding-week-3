# Twitter Threads — Shopify Reviews Article

> **Source:** [final/shopify-reviews-final.md](../final/shopify-reviews-final.md)
> **Format:** 5 standalone tweets, each under 280 characters
> **Tone:** Punchy, direct, merchant-to-merchant

---

**Tweet 1 — The stat hook**

You shipped 200 orders last month. 3 customers left a review.

That's not a quality problem — unprompted review rates sit below 2%.

Your product pages are telling a completely different story than your actual customer experience.

Here's how to fix it 🧵

---

**Tweet 2 — The timing insight**

Most Shopify stores ask for reviews at the wrong time.

Order confirmation? They haven't received it yet.
30 days later? They've forgotten the details.

The sweet spot: 7–14 days after delivery. Fresh enough to remember, late enough to have an opinion.

---

**Tweet 3 — The free win**

The highest-ROI review tactic costs $0:

A short post-purchase email, sent 7 days after delivery, with a direct link to the product review form.

Subject line that works: "How's your [Product Name] working out?"

Feels like a check-in, not a request. Big difference.

---

**Tweet 4 — The negativity bias reframe**

If your reviews skew negative, it doesn't mean your product is bad.

Frustrated customers are self-motivated to write. Happy ones quietly move on.

You're not seeing your real customer sentiment — you're seeing a skewed sample.

The fix: proactively collect from the satisfied majority.

---

**Tweet 5 — The compound effect**

Reviews compound like interest.

The first 10 are the hardest. But once social proof is visible, more customers leave reviews organically — because they see others doing it.

Products with 5+ reviews are 270% more likely to sell (Spiegel Research Center).

Start small. Stay consistent.
